RITUAL ACTIONS
adoratio - kissing the right hand and touching it to the altar.
manus supina – the right hand is raised towards the sky.
liberalitas - the left hand held towards the altar as symbol of generosity.

Intro/Processional/Purification]

Lead 1: Salvete /SAWL-wet-tay/ Citizens and guests! Welcome to Raven’s Cry Grove, ADF, presentation and celebration of Ancient Rome’s spring rite of Quinquatria /kwin-KWAH-tree-ah/, a festival sacred to the goddess Minerva, and held to honor the change of seasons and the hard work of the people of the empire. Our rite is an ADF Druid rite, for in the ways of Rome, rites of groups other than those of the city itself are performed observing their own customs. We shall now mark the space for which our ritual is to be held.
Lead 2: [Mark the four corners of the Nemeton with offerings of water. Follow in the Cardinal Directions, finishing at South.]
We have marked off this space. We have made it sacred. From East to West, from North to South. We claim it as our own. From the here and now, to the fringes of the hills. As the Pomerium protected the Romans, our space is now sacred.
 [Sound 3x3 horn]

Lead 2: Warrior! Call to the Guardian Juno, the watchful mother of Rome.

and prohibit and avert things seen and unseen that may oppose us.
As you protected the people of Rome, we ask you protect us this rite.

Statement of Purpose
Lead 1: Today we gather to celebrate Quinquatria /kwin-KWAH-tree-ah/, the spring equinox festival sacred to Minerva /mi-NAIR-wah/, the goddess of wisdom and skill. This is a time to honor the arts, crafts, and handiworks of the people of Rome. As spring and life come forth, we look to the work and creative energy within. The energy that has not only sustained us but continues to bring us new ideas and more appreciation of the world around us. It is a wonderful time to learn, a time to embrace our skills, and to teach those around us. We share our talents with the kindreds and pay homage to them this day.
Lead 2: We give thanks to the kindreds and praise them this day for the gifts they have given us, may our offerings be accepted, and our prayers be heard.

Main Offering to Minerva

Lead 2: Today we welcome to our ritual, the wise and strong Minerva /mi-NAIR-wah/. Her wisdom in all battles leads us to triumph, her watchful and skillful hand guide all to the creative within.

Minerva: Grey eyed Minerva /mi-NAIR-wah/, goddess of skill and craft.
Great friend you are to the artisan, those who work with the mind and hand.
Born full formed and ready for battle.
To thee we offer these gifts.
Keep our focus strong and lead us as we learn.
Open our minds to new knowledge and prepare us to grow and study.
Oh, Minerva of the owl and shield, accept our offering, and return our blessings in kind.

[Offer woven cloth]

Lead 1: To honor Minerva /mi-NAIR-wah/ this day, and to honor our talents, we shall give Minerva an offering from all of us. We pass around a woven wreath, and a basket of cloth pieces. Please take one piece of cloth. As the wreath is passed to you, tie your cloth to the wreath. Thinking and even speaking of the gifts and skills the gods have given you.
Minerva: [As wreath is passed around, Homeric Hymn to Minerva]
I sing the glorious Power with azure eyes,
Athenian Pallas! tameless, chaste, and wise,
Tritogenia, town-preserving Maid,
Revered and mighty; from his awful head
Whom Jove brought forth, in warlike armour dressed,
Golden, all radiant! wonder strange possessed
The everlasting Gods that Shape to see,
Shaking a javelin keen, impetuously
Rush from the crest of Aegis-bearing Jove;
Fearfully Heaven was shaken, and did move
Beneath the might of the Cerulean-eyed;
Earth dreadfully resounded, far and wide;
And, lifted from its depths, the sea swelled high
In purple billows, the tide suddenly
Stood still, and great Hyperion’s son long time
Checked his swift steeds, till, where she stood sublime,
Pallas from her immortal shoulders threw
The arms divine; wise Jove rejoiced to view.
Child of the Aegis-bearer, hail to thee,
Nor thine nor others’ praise shall unremembered be.

Benediction

The Flamen turns and steps away from the altar, and with left hand in a gesture of liberalitas, right hand raised to the sky manus supina, says:


No more, Gods on High, do I ask of You today; it is enough.

Thus it is done. May all the Gods and Goddesses above and below always love you and wish you happiness in all that is good.

Go now in the peace of the Gods.

May the immortal Gods make it so, as fortunate as it is pious.
