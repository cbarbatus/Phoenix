Emily:
This universe was enveloped in darkness—unperceived, indistinguishable, undiscoverable, unknowable, entirely sunk in sleep. The irresistible self-existent lord, undiscerned, created this universe with the five elements, and all other things were manifest, dispelling the gloom.
He, desiring, seeking to produce life from his own body, first created the waters, and deposited in them a seed. This seed became a golden egg, resplendent as the sun, in which he himself was born as Brahmā /BRA-ma/, the creator of all worlds.  
This wonderful egg, after the Creator had inhabited it for a thousand years, burst open, and Brahmā /BRA-ma/ issuing forth by meditation, commenced the work of creation.
Brahma generated the gods; Brahma generated this entire world. Within him is this entire universe.
Brahmā /BRA-ma/, inspire us as we create our sacred world here this day as you created all the worlds in the beginning!

KEY OFFERINGS


Lisa:  Today, on this Autumnal Equinox, we honor the glorious sun in his incarnations as Surya /SOO-ryah/ and the great Savitr /SA-vee-teh/.
          Surya /SOO-ryah/ is the sun we can see in the sky.  The shining light that fills the heavens with bright golden illumination.  Savitr /SA-vee-teh/ is the sun we cannot see.  The hidden eye.  The divine influence affecting us all from the unknown beyond.  Savitr /SA-vee-teh/ is the rays of the sun peeking over the horizon and the tendrils of the sun sliding away to leave the world in darkness. 
 
Jim: 
Who is Surya /SOO-ryah/, the sun from which these rays precede?

Surya /SOO-ryah/ enlightens the mind and the thoughts with the illuminations of the Truth.

He is vipra /VEE-prah/, the illumined. It is he who delivers the individual human mind from the circumscribed consciousness of self and environment and enlarges the limited movement which is imposed on it by its preoccupation with its own individuality.

Lisa: 
He is bṛhat /BREE-haht/, the Large. But his illumination is not a vague light, nor does his largeness come by a confused and dissolved view of self and object; it holds in itself a clear discernment of things in their totality, their parts and their relations.

Jim: 
He is vipaścit /VIHP-ahsh-kit/, the clear in perception. People as soon as they begin to receive solar illumination, strive to yoke their whole mentality and its thought-contents to the conscious existence of the divine Surya /SOO-ryah/ within them.

They turn all their obscure mental states and erring thoughts to this Light manifested in them so that it may turn the obscurity of the mind into clarity and convert the errors of thought into truths.

 

The yoking becomes their Yoga.


“They yoke the mind, and they yoke their thoughts, the enlightened, so that they may be part of the Enlightened, the Large, the Clear-Perception.”  They seek connection with Surya /SOO-ryah/. 


 

Lisa: 
This leads us to Savitr /SA-vee-teh/. 
The Truth.  He is the Creator, but not in the sense of a fabrication or mechanical forming of things. The root of the word means an impulsion, a loosening forth or sending out—the sense also of the ordinary word for creation and so a production.
The action of the causal idea does not fabricate, but brings out by Tapas /TAH-pahz/, by the pressure of consciousness on its own being, that which is concealed in it, latent in potentiality and, in truth, already exists in the Beyond.
