Setting Historical Precedence – Statement

 

Leader:  Ceres -- the Roman goddess of agriculture, grain, and child bearing. She was a common household goddess, who was loved for her service to mankind. She was credited with teaching humans how to grow, preserve, and prepare corn and grains and the discovery of spelt. She was considered to be responsible for the fertility of the land, worshipped mainly by farmers, and the common wealth. Having a direct role with Romans and their daily life, she was a teacher of man, a nurturer of woman with child, and had the ability to spread or stop famine. With the face of famine threatening Rome, the citizens threatened to rebel, and in 496 BCE, her temple was built on Aventine hill. Alongside her, stand her two children, Liber and Libera who aid her in fertility of the land, wine, and plebeian freedom. Together they were the Aventine Triad, honored with celebrations that lasted  up to a week. tonight, we honor Ceres and her children.

Assistant:  We are here to honor the gods.  So, let us pray with a good fire.  Let us offer with a full heart knowing that when we do, we nurture our relationship with the Kindreds.  In remembering, we strengthen and sustain the gods themselves.  In honor, we balance the worlds, and in love we bring peace and keep the Old Ways alive and present.

Leader:    Here today we honor the gods of Ancient Rome with whom we share existence each day.  We reassure them that, though millennia have passed, they are not forgotten.  We pay special homage today to the triad who were revealed in 496 BCE by the Sibylline Books to be worshipped and thereby end a terrible famine. To bring fertility, enjoyment, and abundance today, we honor Liber Pater [Lee-bear Paw-ter], Libera [Lee-bear-ah], and Ceres [Cair-ace].

Main Offering

Leader: As this day symbolizes the end of the harvest, we will give an offering of grain. Yet we will offer more than just grain. We shall imbue the bread with our own reflections of this year’s bounty and with our prayers of thanks. We will pass this loaf of bread around so that all may lay their hands upon it. Think now on the past year. Think of the joys and the sorrows that it brought. Think of all that succeeded and thrived and all that failed to flourish. Be grateful for the struggles, for they have shaped you and led you to where you are now. You may express your gratitude aloud or silently, as you so choose.

As you do this, think fondly of this rite’s patrons, the representatives of Light and Darkness. They are the balance in our lives.
