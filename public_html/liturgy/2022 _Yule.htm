A word about elfmills...

An “elfmill” is a Swedish folkloric word that basically refers to any place where an elf could bring an item from their world into ours – or vice versa.  The most popular place for an elfmill is one (or several) ground out divots in a stone, also known as “cup marks.” Cup marks are found in stones of every culture worldwide. You may have seen some yourself, locally, at Vasquez Rocks.

In Swedish folk tradition, it is the elves who are making use of these marks, and so the folk leave offerings for them down in the divots; often, they also rub oils around the outside of the divot and chant what they hope the elves will do for them in return. A gift for a gift.

In times long past, folks who weren’t able to travel to one of these “cup mark” elfmills, created elfmills themselves in their very own kitchens by throwing pieces of lead (stolen from church stained glass windows) into boiling water (Note: this is very poisonous. DO NOT EVER DO THIS). They would then leave pieces of jewelry on the created elfmills for a period of time, so that the elves could add their blessings – and then the jewelry was worn or given as charms to those in need of the elves’ magic.

The association of jewelry and metalworking with the elves wasn’t an arbitrary one. The elves – quite specifically the dark elves – were master smithers of all things stone, gem and ore. Everyone knew that the dark elves had crafted many magnificent gifts - many of which were worthy only of the Shining Ones...

Susan/Tale: We have arrived at Yule. The season of offering gifts one to another. To encourage the Svartahlvar [SVART-ahl-var] to receive their offerings, we will construct an elfmill.

Place Lazy Susan with stones on the table.

While we are constructing the elfmill, I will tell you tale...

Place quartz crystals among the stones.

In times of old, in Norway, before electricity and industry... there wasn't a lot to do during this time of year.  After the weapons and tools had been fixed or repaired, new clothing had been made, old clothing mended... and practicing baby making had gone on for long enough, the folk would turn to making gifts to exchange for Yule -  the winter solstice, the turning of the year.  Many of these gifts were made of stone or gem or ore, and who better to call upon for inspiration than the Svartálfar - those crafters of the gifts for the gods:

With each of the next stones, pick it up and present it before adding it to the mill.

Draupnir [DROPE-near]: Odin’s magical ring which produces eight identical rings every ninth night. Add stone with ring to the mill.

Gungnir [GOONG-near]: The head of Odin’s spear which when thrown would never miss its mark. Add stone with spear to the mill.

Gullin-bursti [GOO-lin borst-ee]: Frey’s golden boar which flew across the sky radiating light.
Add stone with boar to the mill.

Skidbladnir [Skihv BLAV-near]: Frey’s ship which always had favorable winds, could sail through the sky as well as water, and even though it could hold all the Aesir and their steeds, it could be folded up and put in a pocket.  Add stone with ship to the mill.

Sif’s [SIF’s] hair: Spun of the finest gold threads. As soon as it touched her head, it grew fast there and became as her own. Add stone with hair strands to mill.

Mjolnir [MULE-near]: Thor’s hammer which hurled at his enemies with destructive power and always returned to his hand.  Add stone with hammer to mill.

Gleipnir [GLAYP-near]: A slender silken rope used to bind Fenris made of, the sound of a cat’s footsteps, a woman’s beard, roots of a mountain, longings of a bear, voice of fishes, and spittle of birds. Add stone with rope to mill.

Tyrfing [TIR-ving]: A magical sword which could cut through iron and stone, fought of its own accord, and once drawn could not be sheathed without tasting blood.  Add stone with sword to mill.

Brisinga-men [BREES-ing-ah-men]: Freya’s necklace, emblem of the stars, and fruitfulness of the earth. It’s beauty so enhanced her charms that she wore it night and day. We won’t go into what she paid for it though.
Add stone with necklace to mill.
