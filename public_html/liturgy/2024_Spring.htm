A bit about American Goddesses

 

Minerva, Columbia and Liberty:

While most of us know Minerva as the Roman goddess of wisdom, justice, law, victory, etc., she is also one of the most prominently featured deities in American iconography. In 1897 a mosaic of Minerva was installed in the Jefferson Building of the Library of Congress, identifying Minerva as guardian of inspiration, wisdom and knowledge in our nation’s capital. 

 

Minerva is also often featured alongside two other American goddesses, Columbia and Liberty. Even if only one of three is featured, there will often be a cross-reference to one (or both) of the other two: Columbia may have Liberty’s broken chains, or Liberty may hold Minerva’s staff, etc. In one piece in particular, Minerva is shown dictating the U.S. Constitution to Columbia!

 

Of more local importance to us, it is Minerva who appears on the California State Seal. Wearing golden armor, with staff and shield in hand, Minerva gazes out over the oceans, landscapes, history and progress of California. The symbol of the bear from our state flag is at her feet. Above her is the word “Eureka”, which means “I have found it.” Inspirational, indeed.

Calafia:

500 years ago, Spanish novelists and explorers imagined a place beyond the Indies - a craggy place, surrounded by ocean but full of rugged hills. It must be a place very close to the region of Earthly Paradise, they said, for those hills had gold - so much gold that they supposed it held no other kind of metal. One Spanish writer in particular named this gold-filled paradise at the edge of the world “California”.  None of them had ever been there, of course.  But that didn’t stop them from writing about those who they thought must be populating the area.  According to the Spanish, California must be entirely populated by strong, black women. Warrior women. Known for hearts that were as valiant as their bodies were strong. Warrior women who created armor from the California gold, and covered their bodies in it, and shone like the sun. 

 

This mythical California was famously ruled by a pagan queen. Her name was Queen Calafia, and she was large and beautiful and in the springtime of her youth. She, too, had a bold and valiant heart, greater than any of those that ruled before her.  Her saga of adventure, war and love became such a popular and well-known story in its time that it earned a passing reference in Don Quixote.

 

Our state is named after this mystical land of pagan warrior women. Queen Calafia, herself, has had a sort of “homecoming” here over the last century. Calafia now symbolically embodies the spirit of the state of California - appearing as such in various pieces of artwork found across the state from San Francisco to Baja. Calafia was portrayed by Whoopi Goldberg in a California Adventure attraction that ran for seven years. The bright pagan queen now even appears as a brand ambassador for almond milk…(!)

 

In 2021, Sausalito staged the "Queen Calafia Welcoming Event", in which the Sausalito Historical Society and local actors depicted the return of Queen Calafia to present-day California, in celebration of her legend and her embodiment as the spirit of our state.

The story

 

Susan:  

 

The Spring Equinox is a time of balance when day and night are of equal length. It marks the awakening of Calafia, the very spirit of California, from her winter slumber. As we make the transition from the shorter days of winter, the Sun climbs higher in the sky, and temperatures start to rise, rousing Calafia with the anticipation of summer.

Calafia has a special relationship with the Sun - for he quite famously dotes on her year-round, even as she sleeps. This makes the equinox, the time of her rising, particularly poignant with the subtle change that begets abundant renewal. The Sun and Calafia, each radiating their own kind of gold, bring life to the coasts, valleys and deserts with vibrantly decorated symbols of rebirth in the blooming yellow-gold beauty of budding oak trees, beach primrose and coastal poppies.

Let us now honor the Increasing Sun and Calafia united in their special moment of renaissance known as the Spring Equinox.

WORKING 

 

The rebirth of the wildflowers will provide nectar for new generations of honey bees, and the renewal of the oak trees in their flowering buds will mature over spring and summer to provide a harvest of acorns in the fall, that will nourish deer, squirrels and many other wild creatures in the darker days of the year.

As we see rebirth and renewal all around us, let us also think about what that means in our own lives.

 

Is there something you want to see grow and bloom?  Maybe it’s a brand new seed that you will need to nurture and raise to the light. Or maybe it’s time for the renewal of an intention or promise that you made in the past, but at some point was forgotten or buried beneath other obligations.  

 

We are passing out little packets of California native wildflower seeds and golf pencils. The back of the seed envelope is blank. Please take a few moments to think about what you want to have bloom and grow in your life this season. Is it the birth of something new? Is it the renewal of an old promise?  When you know what it is, write it on the back of your seed packet.  These could be personal goals, desires, or wishes.

 

When you’ve finished, please look toward me, so that I know you’re all done.

 

(Pause for writing)

         

If everyone is finished, please take that packet of seed with your intention for new birth or renewal on it, and cup it in both of your hands.

Close your eyes and meditate on the energy of spring, the radiant energy of the sun, the growth of new life, and the strength of Calafia. Mighty… Glowing… Strong… Fierce... When you have that feeling of rising energy and growth, visualize the intention that you wrote down coming to fruition, surrounded and empowered by this energy.

(Pause for visualization)

Hold on to that visualization and that energy for a moment. Place that feeling that you have right now, holding this visualization and energy, somewhere in your mind where you can access it again later.  Once you have it, open your eyes. 

 

You are going to take this packet of wildflower seeds home with you.  And you are going to plant them.  What “planting” means - is completely up to you.  If you are someone who likes to garden, you can plant them in the ground, in a pot and tend to them as you do. If you are not a gardener, or don’t have the space to garden, you can take them to a place and scatter them for Calafia to do the rest.  But when you do this, however you do it, bring that energy and visualization you just stored in your mind, bring it back and into the moment.  Once the seeds are dispersed, you can keep the packet somewhere as a reminder of your intention, if that is something that will help you - or you can burn it somewhere safely and release it.
