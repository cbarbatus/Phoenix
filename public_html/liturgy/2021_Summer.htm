INITIATING THE RITE

(Musical Signal Calling the folk to Ritual Stead, 3 horn Blows)

Paul:
At Winter’s end in the old North our far kin welcomed the returning sun. Our ancestors reveled in the short growing season that was to come with the summer and looked forward to a time for travel and trade. These were the days when Kings and jarls dispensed judgments and dooms and dealt important business.

While the folk went about the work of Summer it was said that Freyr /FRRAY-‘r/, frithful Lord of Álfheim [OHLF- hame] rode amongst the world of men upon his golden steed. This was truly Freyr’s /FRRAY-‘rs/ time for these were the days of greatest light, of fertile fields and the mirth and joy that is Summer. Let us now do the same.

Guardian Hallow this Stead!

Opening Statement

Paul:
Statement of Purpose
We gather here to celebrate the Eve of the Summer Solstice in honor of the Lord of
Alfheim: gentle warrior, father, brother, lover fair. It is to give him honors and ask his blessings that we turn our thoughts upon bright Freyr and let his holy light illumine us all.

Lead 2:
Maids traditionally gathered herbs and dew on Midsummer’s Eve and the morning of the Summer Solstice to be used in spells and charms to make themselves beautiful for prospective husbands. Many gazed into the crystal waters of a spring or sacred pool to see visions of their future spouse or what the year would bring to them.

Paul: Midsummer’s Eve is a time of Great magic…the veil that separates the worlds of the Alfar and that of mortals is thin and easy to pass thru. You may use this time to gaze beyond for the answers within. Frey’s Magic is that of compassion and warmth for he teaches of the truest love. With love comes the lesson of resolution, in resolution is peace, in peace is courage and all these things together bring joy. Such are his blessings to all who would seek them.
