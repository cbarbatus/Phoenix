PURIFICATION

Participants are to wash their hands in a bowl of khernip (lustral) water containing a small amount of salt as they enter and are censed.  They are anointed with water on the forehead by a Grove member with an additional bowl of lustral water saying “You may enter in peace”.

As is tradition, the items for ritual are carried in by the folk.  First in, a young girl carries flowers followed by the PDs and then the rest follow in.  A basket of barley with a knife buried inside which will later be used to cut the bread to offer Hestia.

Opening Statement

SARAH:  Khairtete oloi kai Kalos Irthete Adelphia!  Greetings and welcome brothers and sisters!  We come together to celebrate the return of life on Earth, we celebrate the festival of The Chloaia, the verdant green, the returning of the Korē Κορέ [KOR-eh] to her mother Queen Demeter Δήμητρα [DAY-meh-truh]. 

These many cold and rainy months Demeter Δήμητρα [DAY-meh-truh] has mourned and searched for her daughter.  She has searched the ends of the Earth in pursuit of finding her beloved Korē Κορέ [KOR-eh], She has pleaded with mortals and gods alike to aid her in her search.  Yet no solace can she find.  She is consumed and exhausted from her quest.

Demeter Δήμητρα [DAY-meh-truh]’s cries of sorrow have reached the realm of the Underworld where her daughter Persephone is called Queen.  Upon hearing Her mother’s grief, she knows it is time to leave Her dark realm…She feels her mother’s lament in her soul.  She knows her time has come once more to attend to Her Mother and to life on Earth.  She removes her crown, she drinks of the River of Forgetfulness… and so begins her journey back again to the land of the living.  

As She walks green shoots come into sight from deep beneath.  Tender blossoms are ready to burst with color.  We can feel the Earth warming to our touch.  Anxious are the grains to rise up and greet the sun once more as we are anxious for warmth and light after the cold, dark winter.

Marieke: To our Mother Gaia Γαία [YAY-uh], to you we return all of the blessings which we could not carry here.  Uphold us now in this world as you have done in this rite, Σας ευχαριστούμε  [sahs ef-HAR-ess-TOO-muh]; We thank you!
