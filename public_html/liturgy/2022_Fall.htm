INITIATING THE RITE
 
Horn:
(Musical Signal Calling the folk to Ritual Stead, 3 horn Blows?)
 
 
Steven:
Druids! Welcome to the Norse Fall Equinox ritual  Haustblót /HOOST-bloat/ - of Raven’s Cry Grove, ADF and Orange Blossom Protogrove!
 
As Summer comes to an end, the time of reaping, the harvest is upon us. We have watched the seeds we planted in Spring grow and blossom through Summer, and now begin to bear fruit as the days grow shorter. Now the Harvest begins and we gather the fruit of our labor. For our Ancestors in the North, this meant harvesting foodstuffs to last through the harsh wintertime, and the need for abundance to last through that barren time.

The beginning of harvest is a time to honor the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/, the land spirits and deities, the keepers of the land who live among us. We also honor the Kindreds of fertile fields, abundance, joy, and peace.

Jayde:
Feel the pull of our sacred space, calling you, whispering on the wind. Release the cares of your mundane life. Take a deep breath in and allow your mind to be still. In this space we create our sacred realities. We can become all the Kindreds believe us to be. Come forward, be purified and attune yourself to mythic time. Let go and cross the divide.

STATEMENT OF PURPOSE
 
Steven: We gather here today to celebrate the beginning of the harvest season of Autumn. We who live in the realm of Midgard are halfway between the height of summer and the dark of winter, and the days will become shorter as we gather the abundance of our harvest and prepare for the long nights ahead.
 
We feast today in honor of Thor, the God of Thunder who visits us from Asgard /AHSS-gahrd/, who activates the rains necessary for bountiful harvest, and in honor of the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/, our fellow beings on Midgard, who have blessed us through their care-taking during the growing season. We honor all they have done to assist us, and seek their blessing through this season of harvest. This is a time to celebrate before we start the cycle of moving inward until the sun begins to return again. It is a magical time to enjoy the fruit of the earth and connect with our community.

Honor to the Landvættir
Steven: In this Haustblót /HOOST-bloat/, we give special honor to the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/, the beings who care for the land and support our work as we plant, care for, and harvest the fruit of our labor. Without their support, we would not have the joyful flowers, the abundance of grains, fruit, and honey, nor healthy lands on which to live. And we honor them by doing our part to care for the earth, to give offerings to keep all the spirits of the land healthy and happy, and to care for all beings.

Steven: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ of the waters, we honor you for your nourishment and for quenching the thirst of all beings.

Jayde: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ of the mountains and valleys, we honor you for your shade, for the paths you hold for the flow of water, and for landmarks that guide our path and place in the world.

Steven: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ that fly, we honor you for your songs and sounds, for showing us how to be free, and for your communication and pollination between the plants and trees.

Jayde: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ that walk the earth, we honor you for showing us the cycle of life, for the fertilization you provide, and for showing us how to create a home.

Steven: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ of the crops, we honor you for the encouragement of our work to flourish, the protection you provide for our sustenance, and the abundance of our harvest.

Jayde: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ of the wild flora, we honor you for the beauty you bring to our world, ancient spirits of the lands on which we walk and the lands of our ancestors, and for the support you provide the fauna of the land.

Steven: To the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ of our homes, we honor you for the protection you provide for our wellbeing, the safekeeping of our earthly belongings, and the help you provide.

Norse Haustblót Tradition:
Jayde: One tradition during the Haustblót /HOOST-bloat/ Fall Festival is to feast with the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/. The balance and protection they bring to our abundance of harvest and sustenance for the winter, both practically and metaphorically, is key to our survival. It is important we work in harmony with the Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/ in the actions we take throughout the year. But at this time, it is a time to celebrate! A time to feast and partake of the bounty of the year!
Honored Landvættir /LAHND-vie-tihr/, we share this bounty together in your honor as our Ancestors did
(Pass out small bites of bread and apples, leave some apples at the tree)
