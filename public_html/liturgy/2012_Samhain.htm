Opening Statement

KAILA: We gather tonight as a community to celebrate Samhain.  We have reached the most pivotal time of the year, when we are at the end, but also at the beginning of the cycle of the year.  We are at that most sacred point where death and life meet. 

During the pre-Christian times for the Celts, the death of the last harvest and animals meant life by having enough sustenance to survive the Winter.  Obviously, we are fortunate to live in easier times, in the sense that we do not have to slaughter our own food.  However, we are still connected to the ancient ways.

MEG: In Celtic belief and tradition, everything begins in darkness.  All life, a seedling in the ground, a baby in her mother’s womb, gestates and grows in complete darkness. Every single day, we see this idea in action.  How many people here can survive without sleep?  No one can.  We sleep in the darkness so we may rejuvenate and prepare for the next day’s activities.
 
Tonight we invoke the Father of Creation; The Son of the Sea, Manannan Mac Lir. Manannan is the Ferryman who travels through the mist to collect the souls of the newly departed. Then he transports those souls through the veil to the Islands in the West where he sits upon his throne and provides a feast for them at his bountiful table as they take their rightful place as the newest members of our long line of ancestors.

KAILA: It is fitting that this takes place for us at the end of a cycle, and also the beginning of a cycle, as matters of life and death are both ends and beginnings at the same time, and are intimately connected.

Tonight, we invite our beloved Manannan here and ask him to bestow gifts upon us from his Crane Bag. We ask him to part the veil for us so that we may reach out and touch our loved ones on the other side. Tonight with the help of Manannan Mac Lir, we stand at the shore and we honor our beloved Ancestors so that they know that they are deeply loved and respected, and will never be forgotten.

KAILA OFFERS APPLE TO BOAT AND LIGHTS BLUE PILLAR CANDLE IN BOAT.

Tonight we gather at this shore in reverence and in joy. As Manannan pulls back the veil between us, we join with our beloved Ancestors now here with us, to bid farewell to the light time…to bid farewell to the year which has ended and to bid farewell to all that is now lost to us.
Certainly, much has come to each of us throughout the past year. Light….Growth…challenges…productivity….love…fulfillment…..so many gifts, for which we are abundantly grateful to all The Kindred. Tonight at this liminal moment of endings and beginnings, we stand upon this shore, arm in arm with our Ancestors and we are all truly radiant and all truly blessed.


Yet, with all that has come to us, much has been taken from each of us as well.  Such is the way of this life. Beloved family members….friends….pets…many, many people and things, and we grieve those losses.


And there have been other things that have left us as well, for which we do not grieve. Bad habits….bad associations…. negativity…. doubt….pain, things about ourselves for which we feel guilt or shame….. To these things we say good bye and good riddance!


But we must not forget that all of these beings and things are blessings and teachers and we must be grateful for all of them. Tonight in the presence of our wise Ancestors we release the judgments…we release the thoughts of good or bad….and we release the concepts of easy or difficult. We strive to embrace each experience and each person we encounter with a child-like innocence and thirst for knowledge and wisdom.


We bid all that has passed farewell with gratitude as we understand that the challenge of these farewells help us to feel…..and feeling makes us understand how supremely blessed we are to simply LIVE, here and now.


 We each know that surely, in good time The Ferryman will come for each one of us. But this Samhain night we stand united in life, upon THIS, the shore of life, somberly celebrating the magnitude of the transition that we are about to witness.

 
Together, with bittersweet emotion we bid farewell to those beings and things whose time to make the journey into the West, has come.
…And we bless them, and we thank them and thoughts of good and bad fall away as the tears of the living roll like the waves to the shore and the mists enfold us in the arms and the cloak of our most loving and benevolent Father.

 
Manannan, great Ferryman, tonight we gather as a Grove and entrust to you all that has passed from our lives. Open your Crane Bag, bestow your magic upon us and upon our beloved dead.

 
Protector and beneficent host we pray that you ferry them swiftly to the Islands In The West, where they may take their rightful place at your banquet table and feast upon your enchanted and ever rejuvenating swine. Great Oirbsen, only you know the way to Tir na nOg!
 Friends, The time has come. I entreat you to walk the path to the shore. Breathe deeply of the briney sea air.

 
You may have your own offering and that is fine. In addition to your own, we ask that you take a few pumpkin seeds at the shore to offer as well. Breathe your farewell blessing onto those seeds. Send your love and gratitude to the Islands in the West; the place beyond the veil.

 
Seeds might seem a strange offering representing death, however we offer seeds to remind us that just as seeds seem static…dead.…they are filled with the potential for new life. That which may seem to be dead, may well just be dormant now, awaiting transformation into something new when the light returns. In each small pumpkin seed, resides the great mystery of life itself. We do well to contemplate this as we make our offerings and say our goodbyes.

 
Once you have made your offering, please come refresh yourself with a sip of the cool water of creation. Drink so that you can be purified and made fresh in order to go within during the coming dark months. As a caterpillar spins a cocoon and matures in darkness, so do we enter the new year in darkness to go inward, to grow, and to prepare to forge on transformed by our introspection in the coming months.
Come now to Wave Sweeper and bid farewell to what or who has passed. The Ferryman awaits.
