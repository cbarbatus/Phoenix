Opening Statement- PD


“ Croeso y Pob Bendith. Welcome and many blessings. We are here to celebrate the day of Calan Haf, the beginning of summer. This is a day of rejoicing as life continues to flourish all around us. To loosely quote an old Welsh poem;


The beginning of summer, fairest season;
Noisy are the birds, green the woods,
The ploughs are in the furrow, the ox at work,
Blue the sea, the lands are many-colored.


“The world around us is becoming more vibrant as it enters summer. We too are becoming more vibrant. And as the fires of the sun grow stronger in the sky, so too do our own fires grow stronger. It is in this spirit that we gather today to honor Beli Mawr, god of all fires, for he is also the god of the fiery passions we carry inside us. As his powers are renewed through our worship, so are we renewed. His light shines down on the earth, giving it the life and energy needed to sustain every creature. May that same light enter us today to give life and energy to the hopes, dreams, and passions we wish to cultivate this year.


So, on this day, especially this day, let us pray with a good fire.

Earth Mother


PD: “ Let us begin by honoring the Earth, our home.”


" Earth Mother, you are the ground we walk on, build our homes on

and sleep on. You are the sea bordering us, the mountains surrounding us, the sky above us. You provide all and are all. Through you, we are nurtured and grow strong. We give you our love, honor and respect. Tonight, we call on you as Don, Great Goddess of Wales and mother to the Gods. We pray you bless this rite and all that are gathered here.”


“Derbyn ein Hoffrwm! Earth Mother, accept our offering!”


Inspiration


PD: “ And now let us call for inspiration, so that we may speak the rite in beauty”


“ Ceridwen, keeper of the cauldron, amen, source of awen, the fiery waters of inspiration. Changer of shape, bane of Gwion, mother of Taliesin; Come into our grove now and kindle our heads with the brightness of your inspiration so that our words ring sweet in the ears of the powers. Make our voices strong, our minds clear, our bodies able. Take this offering and guide our rite in the way of truth.


“Derbyn ein Hoffrwm! Ceridwen, accept our offering!


Bydded Felly!”


II. Opening of the Grove


Grove Attunement- Two powers centering To be done by PD.


The Hallows


PD: “ Let us sanctify the hallows by which we make offering.”


Well

“ Ffynnon Sanctaidd, Sacred well. Waters of Annwfn. Portal to the worlds below. Well of dark wisdom. Blood of the earth. Source of the rivers, streams, lakes, oceans, and the waters that flow under the earth. We call on you so that the dark realms of the beloved ancestors, spirits, and cythonic gods be brought here and channeled through you this evening. We make our call with bright silver so that your waters may be a shrine for our worship.


Ffynnon Sanctaidd, llifa o’n mewn! Sacred Well, flow within us!”


Fire


“ Dan Sanctaidd, Sacred Fire. Holy light of the shining ones. Embodiment of inspiration. Fire in the head. Element of controlled chaos. You are the fires of the hearth, of love, passion, and inspiration; the bright heat of the sun and the cool light of the stars and moon. You are the bright essence of Beli Mawr. We call on you so that the bright places where the shining, celestial gods and spirits abide be brought here and channeled through your flames. We make our call with sweet smelling oil so that your flames may be a shrine for our worship.


Dan Sanctaidd, llosga o’n mewn! Sacred Fire, burn within us!”


Tree


“ Coeden Sanctaidd, Sacred Tree. Boundary of all worlds. Creature rooted deep and crowned high. Sacred Pillar. Carry us with you to your depths and raise us up to your heights. Be for us the center of the grove, the hub of the wheel, connecting us to all the worlds. We make our call to you with sacred water and sacred smoke and ask that you be the center of this place.


Coeden Sanctaidd, tyfa o’n mewn! Sacred Tree, grow within us.”


Opening the Gates


PD: “ We have sanctified the hallows. Now let us call on the Gate Keeper to bring the worlds together.”


“ Gwyn ap Nudd, Lord of the Otherworld. God of life and death, light and dark. King of the Tylwyth Teg. We call on you. Emerge from the deep unknown. Ride out of the obscure mist. Soar out of the dark woods, where you take the dead to your court under the hill. Rise up from the darkness and come into our grove! Your child calls on you to make the way ready for us. Come to us, with your blackened face and dapple-gray horse, with your ghostly hounds trailing behind you. Meet us at the boundary of our hearts, and of this place, where the elements meet in balance. Ward us, so that we walk the ways in safety.


Share your magic with ours as we open the ways.

Let us, together, make the well a gate to the worlds below!

Let us, together, make the fire a gate to the worlds above!

Let us, together, make the tree the crossroads of all the worlds!


Agorer Y Pyrth! Let the Gates be opened!




III. Offering to the Powers


Honoring and Invoking the Powers


PD: “ Let us now call on the kindreds so we may honor them.”


Nature Spirits


“ Ysbrydion Natur, Nature Spirits. Spirits who share this world with us.

Children of the green earth. Animals of fir, fin, feather, and scale. Creatures of leaf, flower, rock, and bark, The Tylwyth Teg and faery of all lands. You are our brothers and sisters, companions and allies. We offer you welcome. Noble Ones, we give you this offering of fruits and flowers and ask that you join us at our hearthside this evening.


Ysbrydion Natur, derbyn ein hoffrwm! Nature Spirits, accept our offering!


Bydded Felly!


Ancestors


“ Hynafiaid, Ancestors. Those whom we are related to in blood and those that we revere for their gifts to the world. Great peoples of days now long gone and dear ones that we have lost recently. Ancient people of these lands, whose bones are buried in it and whose hearts are tied to it. On this evening we remember you and give you due honor. You are our forbearers and we are your legacy. Mighty Ones, we give you this offering of grain and ask that you join us at our hearthside.


Hynafiaid, derbyn ein hoffrwm. Ancestors, accept our offering!


Bydded Felly!


Deities


“ Dduwiau, gods and goddesses of the House of Don and gods and goddesses of the House of Llyr, as well as the patron gods of all those gathered here. Eldest and wisest of spirits, greatest of guides, best of friends, and most nurturing of parents. Shining ones, through you we are blessed, and through your blessings and lessons, we too learn to shine. You are the origins of all things and the most worthy of our love, respect, and devotion. Shining Ones, we give you this offering of whiskey and ask that you join us at our hearthside.


Dduwiau, derbyn ein hoffrwm. Shining Ones, accept our offering!

Bydded Felly!

Main Invocation and Offering


PD: Brothers and sisters, we have opened the gates, called the kindred, and given them

offerings with love in our hearts. It is now time to call on the deity of this rite, Beli Mawr.


Bright, shining one, first father and first son, crowned with flame. Fire that wakes the womb. Your light is the source of courage, wisdom, passion, strength, love and desire. You are the fires of creation and also destruction. In the beginning of the year, we honored your powers of destruction and the darkness of that time when we remember those that have passed on to be consumed by your flames and returned to the earth. Now is the time of the light half, the beginning of summer, and we honor your powers of creation. We call to you so that we may honor you, father of kings, that you might grow stronger, that you might grow brighter. May our words and actions please you and bring your attention to us, that the warmth of this fire may be the warmth of your affection for us. Now, hear these sweet words sung for you, Bright Beli, that you might grace us with your presence.


Calon Lan to be sung at this point.


Bydded Felly!


Beli the Great is now among us. Feel his heat in the fire before you and feel his heat in the fires in your heart. Rejoice that as the days pass on, he grows stronger, for it is now summer. This is the time of the bright, fertile half of the year. This is the beginning of the summer’s labor, when we work and put energy into our passions, projects, and livelihood in hopes of harvesting them at a later time. At this time and all times, it is the blessings of Beli Mawr that we depend on. And so on this day of celebration, there is no greater gift we can give to Beli for all the bounty he gives us than our energy through our movement and dance. Like our ancestors before us, let us dance around the fire. Let us sway and move, spin and shout. Do this in gratitude of the bright king, offer it all as a gift to him!


As you dance, I will gather the energy we send out into this wreath to be consumed by the flames. As you dance, send your energy, love, and devotion into the wreath, or if you like, the fire itself.


Dancing and music to be done here to raise energy.


Bright Beli, your children have danced around your fires in your honor. Our actions have been your entertainment and our devotion your nourishment, consumed by your flames. May you be pleased by the honor we give you.


Beli Mawr, Derbyn Ein Haberth! Beli Mawr, accept our sacrifice!
