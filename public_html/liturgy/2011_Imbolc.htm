Opening Statement
PD: This Rite of Imbolc, that we celebrate today, what is it?  It is a new cycle of birth, the rich warmth of mother’s milk, hope, preparation, a fire in the cold, silent nestling-smiles, the spark of life that uplifts tired hearts, that sings new stirrings in our veins.

 

And who is She, honored in many tribes and traditions?  Goddess of Divine Inspiration and Prophecy, Shaper of Destiny and Deeds, Fire Healer, Honor Song, Home Tender, Cauldron Keeper, Paragon Muse, Guardian of Women and a Soft Lap for Children.  A Brid, Bridget, O Bride and Blacksmith – it is Herself.  Today, we welcome her promise and her arrival for as she steps into the path of this time, our instincts hunger for the spark of renewal that is taking place within us and within the earth.  Like seeds in the deep earth, we are moving and growing, vibrating in anticipation, awakening to our little dreams longing to push their way to the surface.   The sun begins its return to warm our hope and to alight in us new possibilities.  So let us stand here today as one people to ponder our purpose for this year and to honor the gifts of Brid that we will commit to bring forth. 

 

As our Ancestors have done before, and as we do now, may our children continue to remember the Kindred and celebrate the mysteries of Life, Death, and Renewal.
