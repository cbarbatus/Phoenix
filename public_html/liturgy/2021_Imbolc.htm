Statement Of Purpose

Sarah: We gather here to celebrate Imbolc, the time before the birth of lambs when the ewes come into their milk. For some, Imbolc remains a day frozen in the grip of winter, when half the stores are gone, and the land is still bleak and quiet. But here, in this land, we recognize the stirring of life beneath our feet, as winter begins to depart, as the rivers flood, as the days begin to lengthen, as the first buds appear on the trees, and tulips push through the cold soil to greet the sun.
 
Elizabeth: We are here to offer worship to the triple goddess Brighid: She who protects, heals, and inspires. Renewal begins for mother earth; seeds take root and come to flower. As the light strengthens and begins to awaken the land, let it be so for us as well; let us clear away the old, dead leaves of winter and prepare our soil to be planted anew, fresh and clear with intention and ripe with purpose.
 
Sarah: As our ancestors did, so do we now: we are here to honor the Old Bargain between mortals and the Kindreds. So let us join together as one folk to make our offerings in joy and reverence.

Elizabeth: Children of the earth, it is time to meet the Goddess Brighid.
 
Stand, sit, or lie down in a comfortable position. Take in a deep breath, allowing the element of air to cleanse you of your troubles of the day. Squeeze, every muscle in your body, hold it, now release. Take a second breath, allowing yourself to release any tension in your body. Now take a third breath, and see yourself in a beautiful field, dense with green grass and wildflowers. There are cows mooing in the distance. It’s a beautiful night, the moon is full and bright.
You walk a little and discover that you’re at a doorway to a brightly lit house. You knock, and the door opens on its own. You see a beautiful woman with red hair standing before you, smiling warmly.  Her home is a unique combination of comfort and beauty. You see a warm fiery cauldron and, surprisingly, a well inside her house. She has smithing tools, musical instruments, an immense apothecary with all the things that heal and do great works of magic. It’s Brigid - Bride, Brigantia, or Brigitte to some, St. Brigid to others - a Goddess of many names, worshiped by many different people.
“Welcome”, she says, inviting you to sit down. “I’m glad you’re here. You are on a journey, and I would like to help you along your path.”

Once you sit down, you feel a fire in your belly. The fire grows, expanding out to your sides, up your torso, down your arms and legs. It burns away all that you no longer need - the worries and cares of the world, the wounds and the limiting beliefs that serve no longer - transforming you. Then you feel healing water being gently poured over you, quenching the fire and healing the places where you need healing. All that no longer serves you is washed down into the earth.
A golden light appears and surrounds you like a bubble. You start to see words and phrases appear - words of inspiration and validation. These words are for you.
Then she asks, “What would you like to know, that will help you along your path? Ask me anything. I will speak to you more about this in your dreams. Listen for me, and I’ll be there.”
“What tool would you like to have to help you along your journey?” She gives it to you. “How would you like to be healed, to strengthen you on your journey?” It is done.
“What is it, that you would like to achieve this season, that will put you further along in your journey? Close your eyes and see it clearly, what it looks like, what it feels like. It is yours. As above, so below! If you need me, for protection, or further insight, or healing, if you need me call on me, and I will be there.”
You open your eyes. The house is gone. The cows are gone. There is only the moon. And yet, you still know what she told you, you are still transformed and healed, you still have the tool she gave you, and your success is imminent. Bring yourself back to this ritual space, bringing all Brigid has given you.

THE TALE OF BRIGID’S MANTLE

Lisa: ‘Mantle’ is another word for cape, or cloak...
In one of the well-known tales about Brigid, she felt called to establish a retreat, a center of welcome, hospitality and peace where all people, especially the poor, could come and find nourishment for life’s journey.  She went to the king of Leinster [LEN-ster] to ask him for some land on which to establish her center.  She told him that the place where they stood was the perfect spot: it was beside an oak grove where she and her companions could collect firewood and berries, there was a lake nearby that would provide water, and the land was fertile, good for raising sheep and cows.
The king, not renowned for generosity, was hesitant and asked her how much land she’d need. “Oh, just as much as I can cover with my mantle,” replied Brigid. This meager request pleased the king, so he readily agreed. Thereupon Brigid took off her mantle and laid it on the ground.  And miraculously, the mantle began to GROW! It grew, and it grew, and much to the king’s astonishment, it continued to grow, until it covered several acres of rich and beautiful land.  The king was dismayed, but honor prevented him from going back on his word. And so was established the town of Cill Dara /Kill DAH-ra/, Irish for ‘the church of the oak’, present-day Kildare, /Kill DARE/ where to this day the Brigidine [BRIDGE-ih-DEEN] sisters keep alight the perpetual flame dedicated to Brigid.
