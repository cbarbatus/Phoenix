Norse Yule Ritual

Raven’s Cry Grove, ADF

Written by Jason Altobelli and Heather Marriner

For public rite January 7, 2012

 

Prep for the Hof:

4 chairs on the far wall, sitting on the dais: from left to right, the Cupbearer's, the Lords, the Lady's, and the Thul's. The bards are set up in the corner. Two tables are placed in front of the dais, with a 10ft. or so tablecloth. This is the stalli(altar), and all ritual implements are placed on it: horn, 3 covered god statues, garlands, offerings, troth ring, hlaut bowl, hlaut sprinkler, rune bag.

 

There is an empty space in the center of the table where the lamb will be.

 

The High Seat pillars (ondvegisullur) are lying on the ground in the center of the room, covered in nice cloths.

 

Food prep is in the kitchen. We need good food. Wassail would be great.

 

 

Call to Protector / The Sith

 

Mike is inside in the room adjacent to the main room, ready to go. Russell and Meg are in the skald's corner, ready to start playing as people process in.

 

The Thorr god statue is sitting outside by the tree. Darin stands at the tree.

 

Folk gather in the outside courtyard in one line The Cupbearer goes down the line and gives everyone an unstained white cloth and a candle.

 

JASON: “Everyone, thanks for coming to Yule. A few matters of protocol before we begin. Both ADF and  Heathen custom dictate that we hail aloud only Norse gods during blot. And one additional point: please don't hail Loki, Hel, Fenriswolf, or other baleful wights during this ritual. Thank you. With that said, let us celebrate Yule in frith.”

 

The Lady of the Hall and a Doorkeeper are waiting at the doorway and do not process. The Doorkeeper holds a wet towel and a bowl of water.

 

JASON: Nine rings on a bell.

 

Darin removes the covering from the Thorr statue.

 

DARIN: “Terrible Thorr, Red-bearded jotunn-bane, protect our rite from evil. Veurr-Thor, strong guard of the shrine, gast-slayer, we give you offering.”

 

Darin pours beer over the statue.

 

The Thul lights his candle (or a larger fire in a bowl if possible), holds it in his hands,.

 

JASON: "As once was done in ancient times, we take land for our own by fire. I kindle this flame with Ving-thor. Thorr, hallow this hof. Make and take this place for the Aesir and the folk. Thorr vigja ok helga thetta hof." ("Thorr hallow and protect this holy hof.")

 

The Cupbearer takes a light from the Thul's candle/bowl and lights the candles all down the line. She then takes her place at the front of the line.

 

The Thul leads the Folk around the courtyard. The Thul chants "Thorr vigja ok helga thetta hof" during the procession.

 

Purification

 

The Thul leads the Folk back to the doorway. Everyone's still in line. Inside, Russell and Meg start the music.

 

REN: “As we process into the hof, please clean your hands in the water. Once inside, please place your candle on a shelf and stand in front of a seat. Now, allow yourself to let go of all mundane things...insert grounding....now we are ready to worship as a community.”

 

The procession begins. The Thul greets the Lady warmly, then goes in. The next person is greeted by the Lady, and if all's well, she allows him in. So on and so forth. Chat between Lady and guest should be short, 10 seconds at most. The point of this is to make the participants feel like participants, not audience members. Meanwhile, the Doorkeeper is offering the towel and water so that everyone cleanses their hands before entering.

 

The Thul enters the Hof and remains standing. The Folk follows and remain standing. Heather, the Cupbearer, and the Doorkeeper enter and stand by their seats.

 

Mike as the Lord of the Hall enters from another room.

 

JASON: “Hail the Lord of the Hall!”

 

Everyone says Hail!

 

Mike sits down and directs everyone to do the same. Everyone else takes their seats.

 

Statement of Purpose

 

JASON: "Hail to the Gathered Folk! We are Raven's Cry Grove and we welcome you to Yule. Let us give to the gods! Let us swear on the bragarfull and the boar! Will you give well? (wait for response) Then we will surely gain a great year.

 

This season is the most important time of the year. Our ancestors came together at this time because it was a natural break in the work year, and thus natural for communion. No farming (and little raiding) could be accomplished in this cold and desolate season. People were drawn indoors, and as they came together, they brought to the table their combined experiences, their angers, troubles, and joys of years past and also years to come.

 

With so much urdr together under one roof, it was impossible to separate people from their deeds, from their past glories as well as their mistakes. The act of coming together, of becoming less about individuals and more about a community, created strong bonds between men. These bonds echo through time. Maybe that's why both whispers from the past and wishes for the future seem to loom large at Yule: it's a time of endings and beginnings, our dead are remembered and thus given life again, old accounts and pacts are shored up, and new ones are created. Our Gods draw close: as we pile high the fruits of our labor, as we reunite with our brothers, we cannot fail to remember their ancient deeds, deeds that gave us light to see by, land to grown on, and the order in which we prosper.

 

But when we look outside, we see freezing death! We hear the howling of wolves! Surrounding our small fires are the ravagings of chaos: draugr leave the mounds and walk again, thurs, gast, and unmentionable things haunt the long darkness. Even our own One-Eye leads his furious host across the skies, his terrible call a reminder that no boon, no matter how fervently petitioned, is ever guaranteed.

 

That's why this is the most important time of the year. Urdr is becoming orlog. We have gathered here, and our pasts and futures are here with us. Both our Gods AND their enemies draw close, and are futures are being decided in the space between them. The awful AND the holy are here. Maybe they are the same.

 

It can be hard to remember this, living on land that seems like another planet compared to our ancestral homes, and with the majority of our work happening behind computer screens in heated rooms, but it's not up for debate. These are the cycles of our lives, and we can't escape them.

 

So let's do what we do best: give to the gods that give us their blessings in return. Just remember that your words and actions affect us all. Make no unwise boasts and when called to speak, choose your words carefully.

 

Hail to the mighty Aesir, to the Grove and the Folk, hail to our leaders past and present, and hail to our hof!"

 

Everyone says Hail!

 

Honoring the Earth Mother

 

JASON: "Let us begin. It is our custom and thew to honor the land, our Mother."

 

Kaila comes up,

 

KAILA: "Revere the bounteous Earth! Snow-hooded one, grown with oaks, tressed with grasses. We give thanks to our land, the birther of our harvests."

 

Kaila gives offering in front of the Tree.

 

Everyone says Hail!

 

Creating the Cosmos/Inviting the Three Kindred

 

The Well/Ancestors

 

RUSSELL: "It is a thew of our Grove to invite the Ancestors through the Well, this urn of water in which we leave offering. Ancestors, welcome back from your dark halls. Like the well of Mimir you bring us your wisdom and power from days long past. May we learn and grow from your knowledge. Hear us this night and always. Hail the Ancestors, hail the Alfar and the Disir!

 

Everyone says Hail!

 

Russell offers to the Well.

 

The Tree / Nature Spirits

 

MEG: "It is a thew of our Grove to invite the Land Spirits with the Tree, this hallow around which we will leave offering. Just as Yggdrasil is the axis of all the worlds, you are the attendants and stewards of all the worlds. May we learn from you vigilance. Hear us this night and always. Hail Landvaettir!

 

Everyone says Hail!

 

Meg offers to the Tree.

 

The Fire:

 

HEATHER: "It is a thew of our Grove to invite the Gods with the Fire, this warming light around which we will leave offering. Reginn, we tend our hearth fires to bring warmth and light to our homes, but it's you who have kindled our fires since before we born. May we always shine bright in your honor! Hear us this night and always. Hail Reginn!

 

Everyone says Hail!

 

Heather offers to the Fire.

 

JASON: "All holy wights, be welcome as we make our hof ready for blot!"

 

The Gates:

 

JASON: "In the past, holy spaces were permanent. Heathens made them holy by years of blood sacrifice, hard work, and observance of ritual taboo. It is a thew of our Grove that, as we have no permanent temple, we make whole and holy our temple with a ritual act. Tonight we'll make this place fit for the wights we call and establish our grove's troth by raising the ondvegisullur, the high seat pillars.

 

Surrounding the high seat of the chieftain, Ondvegissulur are the sacred steads of a kindred group. When a group migrated, they were often the first structures to be erected in a new settlement. We even remember Thorolfr Mostrarskegg who threw his own high seat pillars overboard as he neared Iceland, trusting in the gods to direct him well. He settled where they came to shore.

 

As our skalds sing poetry as offering and thanks to our Gods for the season, we will raise our pillars here and make this space holy.”

 

Sarah, Jackie, and Tom uncover the pillars and raise them. Skalds play Snow. Kaila goes to the kitchen  now to prepare the meat. After the pillars are raised, others come and start to decorate the pillars with stick-on garlands, put small offerings around them, maybe whisk some incense around them. MAKE THIS PART LAST THE WHOLE SONG. 

 

JASON: “Our hof pillars are raised and we are ready to sacrifice.”

 

Blot

 

The Lord and the Thul approach the stalli.

 

The Thul addresses the folk, the Lord remains picks up the hlaut bowl and the hlaut sprinkler. The Lord  picks up the troth ring and puts it on now.

 

JASON: "Heill Gothanum! We blot to the great Yule gods as once was done. To the gods we offer the hallowed blood of boar, bought with our funds and dedicated to the Reginn. Great Ones, receive your sacrifice!"

 

Kaila brings the meat from the kitchen in from the kitchen and lays it on the altar. She then takes the meat drippings (make sure there's a TON of blood in it) and ladles them into the hlaut-bowl.

 

The Thul uncovers the Othinn statue.

 

JASON: "Hail to the Hanged One! Hail Othin! Flashing-eyed frenzied Host, Wod-stirrer, Spear-Inviter. We hail you as the Wishfather, and we give you the blood you delight in. Give us victory in the coming year!

 

Othinn vintu taka vith thetta frá oss! Heil Othinn!"

 

Lord sprinkles hlaut over the Othin statue.

 

The Thul uncovers the Njorth statue.

 

REN: "Hail to the Giving God! Lord of shorelines and lover of swan calls, you of the beautiful feet, ruler of high-timbered Noatun, we give you due offering. Give us wealth in the coming year!

 

Njorth vintu taka vith thetta frá oss! Heil Njorth!"

 

Lord sprinkles hlaut over the Njorth statue.

 

The Thul uncovers the Frey statue.

 

HEATHER: "Hail to the Harvest God! Sun-golden boar lord, giver of rain and sun, antler-bearer, we give you due offering. Give us peace and health in the coming year!

 

Frey vintu taka vith thetta frá oss! Heil Frey!"

 

Lord sprinkles hlaut over the Frey statue.

 

JASON: “Hail the Yule Gods! As well, we make whole and holy our troth with the gods by hallowing our temple and our high seat pillars.”

 

Lord then sprinkles the Well, Tree, and Fire (in that order) with hlaut, then hits everything on the stalli except the horn. Finally he sprinkles the pillars.

 

JASON: “Gathered folk and Grove, if you have offering for the your gods and spirits, come and offer them now.”

 

They do.

 

JASON: "Finally, gathered folk, you share in the hlaut-blessing. If you accept, hold out your cloths and receive it. Ek gefa þer Reginn blessun!" (I give you the blessings of the Reginn).

 

Mike then passes the hlaut-bowl and -sprinkler to Ren, who goes around the Folk and smears the white cloths given to each guest (if they accept). Skalds are playing meanwhile.

 

Omen

 

Lady of the Hall casts the Runes. One for the Ancestors, one for the Nature Spirits, and one for the Gods.

 

Lady of the Hall asks if the Folk accept the blessing. If they do, she puts the runes away and sits back down.

 

Sumble

 

Heather goes to the altar and fills the horn with mead.

 

HEATHER: "This horn is filled with the gifts of our mighty gods. Let us begin the holy rite of sumble. We'll pass the horn three times in honor of the gods,  our ancestors, and to everything else good and worthy in our lives. First let us give honor to the Aesir. If the Cupbearer offers you the sweet mead, should you feel called, speak a few worthy words in praise of the Gods of the North. Feel free to simply say 'Hail the Gods' as well!

 

And, let us remember our ancestors. If the Cupbearer offers you the sweet mead, speak a few worthy words about your kin now resting in the mounds. Please make sure not to hail anyone still living.

 

And, now let us remember our ancestors. If the Cupbearer offers you the sweet mead, speak a few worthy words about your kin now resting in the mounds. Please make sure not to hail anyone still living."

 

The Lady of the Hall then passes the horn to the Cupbearer. Instead of one person passing the horn to another as we usually do, Ren gives it to everyone individually, and takes it back afterward. After every toast, everyone says “Hail!”

 

The Lady of the Hall leaves the horn on the stalli.

 

JASON: “Gathered folk, do you accept these words? Then may they prove true! We have honored our gods, remembered our dead, and confronted the year ahead of us. Heilsan the gathered folk! Let us bring our rite to a close.”

 

Thanking the Beings

 

JASON: "The offerings have been received and the gods have heard us. We call once more to the holy wights."

 

Othinn:

 

JASON: "One-eyed One, for your gifts and blessings, ek thakka ther fyrir, we thank you."

Everyone says “we thank you!”

 

Njorth:

 

REN: "Giving Njorth, for your gifts and blessings, ek thakka ther fyrir, we thank you."

Everyone says “we thank you!”

 

Frey and all Reginn:

 

HEATHER: "Harvest Frey, and all holy Reginn whether Aes or Van, for your gifts and blessings, ek thakka ther fyrir, we thank you."

Everyone says “we thank you!”

 

Thorr:

 

DARIN: "Vingthor, for your gifts and blessings, ek thakka ther fyrir, we thank you."

Everyone says “we thank you!”

 

Landvaettir:

 

MEG: "Landvaettir, for your gifts and blessings, ek thakka ther fyrir, we thank you."

Everyone says “we thank you!”

 

Alfar and Disir:

 

RUSSELL: "Alfar and Disir, for your gifts and blessings, ek thakka ther fyrir, we thank you."

Everyone says “we thank you!”

 

Earth Mother:

 

KAILA: "Thank you as well to our land, for your continual gifts and blessings. We give you what remains of our offerings and blessings."

Everyone says “we thank you!” Kaila pours out whatever remains of the horn's contents outside.

 

Says the Thul:

 

JASON: “We have given to our Holy Ones, and a good year begins. In thanks, and in preparation for our feast, let us sing once more. The lyrics to Lifeblood are on the back of your foldout. As we begin the feast, let us pay our final respects to the Great Gods of Yule!”

 

Darin does the THUM, THUMTHUM rhythm twice, and we start the song.

 

We all do the Lifeblood song  

 

Lifeblood

Drink for the wind blows cold and
Drink for the Wolf runs free

for Othinn's sons are we!

 

 

Then we feast! Grove members bring out the food. After the feast is over and people leave, the Thul takes down the pillars in a reverent fashion and begins to clean up.

 

 

 

Glossary:

 

hof – temple

sith- procession

Thul – speaker (sometimes challenger)

Ondvegissulur- high seat pillars

stalli – altar

hlaut – hallowed blood (note: this will be meat drippings, not straight-up blood)

troth – our bond and contract with the gods and each other

orlog – the path our lives take, the future the Norns weave out of urdr (aka wyrd)

Jotunn – giant, like our Fomorians

Veurr – byname of Thorr, “guardian of the shrine,” maybe

Gast – ghost or other horror

Vingthor - “Giving Thor”

Bragarfull – oathing over a cup of something

Wyrd – fate (long story)

Thurse – lower-level giant, more like a troll

Aesir – one of the two tribes of Gods who give to us and protect us.

Mimir – keeper of a well at the foot of Yggdrasil, a well full of wisdom

Yggdrasil – world tree

Disir – female ancestors

Alfar – technically “elves”, but often associated with male ancestors or ancestors in general

Landvaettir – land spirits

Reginn – a more general term for the gods that give to us and protect us

Sigrfodr - “wish-father,” byname of Othinn

Wights – living things, corporeal and not

Blot – a blood sacrifice

Sumble – three rounds of ritual toasting

Minni - “remembrance,” a round of the sumble for your ancestors
