Mike:  Lughnasadh

Is the first of two harvest festivals, the second being Alban Elfed (the Autumn Equinox). At Lughnasadh we see the fields of corn being cut, and for some this is the true time of the festival. In the fields John Barleycorn, who laid with the Lady in the woods at Beltane, has grown old, and now stands bent and bearded with a crocked cane. He looks to the Sun as he has changed from green to gold, and he known that his time has come. His life will feed the people, and it is this sacrifice that we honour at Lughnasadh.

 

Lugh dedicated this festival to his foster-mother, Tailtiu, the last queen of the Fir Bolg, who died from exhaustion after clearing a great forest so that the land could be cultivated. When the men of Ireland gathered at her death-bed, she told them to hold funeral games in her honor. As long as they were held, she prophesied Ireland would not be without song.

 

Tailtiu’s name is from Old Celtic Talantiu, "The Great One of the Earth," suggesting she may originally have been a personification of the land itself. In fact, Lughnasadh has an older name which refers to the painful labor of childbirth. For at this time of year, the earth gives birth to her first fruits so that her children might live.

Main Offering

 

Assembled stand in a circle around the 4 treasures, which are in the middle with a hole dug in the center of the four. Folk slowly walk counterclockwise quietly chanting “Key-Ah” for one full revolution. All stop as Leader steps into the circle, walks to the Spear and says:

 

Kaila: 

Spear of the South – of Gorias…spear of fire….Behold, The Spear of Lugh that never misses the mark!


The spear of Lugh carries far more weight than just a spear of battle or a spear of the hunt. To wield this spear is to have laser-like focus and single-minded aim. Direct action. Channeled attention. This spear speaks of beginnings. It stands tall and straight, saying NUMBER ONE – the beginning of all series of counts.

 

This spear speaks of the divine male - erect - and when engaged, passionately thrusting. Just as the male phallus is the tip of conception - so too does the tip of this spear meet with its target as a sign of contact, making an opening, prompting a beginning leading to action and creation.

 

Spear of Lugh, bestow upon each of us your passion and creativity; bring renewed creation to each of us by means of inspired action! (Anoint with rose water)

 

leader moves to Lia Fail and says:

 

Stone of the North, Stone of Falias……Stone of ordination. Behold the Noble Lia Fail, the Stone that knows the heart of humanity!

 
The High Kings of Ireland were ritually married to the goddess living in this Stone of Destiny. Before the nuptial the High King would be summed up by the goddess of the Stone. If his measure was lacking, the goddess embodying the stone would scream her displeasure. Thus, the Lia Fail would predict the next King.

This beautiful stone is deceptive in her stillness. In truth she is plugged in to the belly of the Earth Mother and is a conduit for high voltage energy. The Lia Fail has the power to rejuvenate the king and endow him with a long reign.

Earth Icon, Lia Fail, Stone of Ireland, bring us closer to Our Mother and fill us with her Regal presence. Lia Fail, through your ancient widsom show us that we are each the true sovereigns of our own lives!  (Anoint with rose water)

leader moves to The Sword and says:

Sword of Nuada, sword of the East – of Findias…..sword of transmission - All are subject to the will of the Sword.

Ancient Nuada was the king of the Tuatha de Danann, and so, this - his sword wields great power.  This sword is the symbol of the testing of Mettle under duress. Because it is wielded through the air to meet it’s mark against enemies, this sword is also the symbol of communication with a focus on purity of intent.

 

Battle is an honorable act, intended for the purpose of cutting through obstacles to gain higher ground. And so we sing to all heros and warriors and through this sword we honor their sacrifice, and their effective rulership through purity of intent.

 

Sword of Nuada, cut through the conflicts we encounter in this life. Bestow upon us the spirit of the warrior. Bestow upon us purity of intent. Bestow upon us the wisdom to wield our power effectively in order to achieve clarity and truly become effective rulers of our own lives.  (Anoint with rose water)

 

leader moves to The Cauldron and says:

 

Cauldron of the Dagda, Cauldron of the west – of Murias…..Cauldron of abundance, Cauldron of endless supply, Cauldron from whom no one goes away unsatisfied.

 

 

 

The cauldron of the Dagha is the cauldron of nurturing, nourishment and replenishment. It brings fertility and abundance, renewal and rebirth. Those wounded in battle are restored to health by being dunked into the magical curative waters within this vessel.

 

Magical Cauldron of the great Daghda, nurture and restore us within your deep, watery recesses. Bestow upon us never ceasing abundance. Make each of our hearts your Cauldron so that we may abundantly provide for, nurture and restore those wounded warriors both within ourselves and in this world each day. (Anoint with rose water)
