Statement of Purpose

Elizabeth: Today we gather between the Earth and Sky. To worship and honor the Kindred as one people. To sing their praise and make our sacrifice. As we seek their blessings in return.

 

Mike: Today we celebrate Lughnasadh /LOO-nuh-suh/. A time in ancient days when the Tribes of Ireland would gather together, put aside their differences, and honor the gods and one another. As we join together today, we are reminded of this tradition. Though we all may come from different walks of life, we all gather as one tonight to honor the Kindreds. We reflect this gathering in our offerings tonight, as Lughnasadh was the start of the harvest, we offer grains from across the world. Symbolizing the unity of all our lands and people. Let us join in reverence, Bíodh sé amhlaidh! /BEE-uh SHAY OW-lee/!

Invocation and Offering to Lugh

 

Mike: Now, we call upon the all skilled one, our Grove patron, Lugh. And together, we shall all honor him!

 

Helpers pass out dried parchment sheets and writing tools to the folk, one of each.

 

Elizabeth: Druids, as we pass them out, please take one parchment paper sheet and one writing tool. Share if you have to.

 

Wait a few moments for folk to receive materials.

 

Elizabeth: Lugh is a god of skills, personal achievements, and champions. He is honored through acts of bravery, heroics, and victories. What better way is there to offer to him tonight than with our own accomplishments? Druids, reflect on your year so far. What triumphs have you earned? What acts of bravery have you done? What personal successes have you had? They do not have to be grand gestures, unless you have had one, they can be small. Anything from advancement in your faith, schooling, or career to passing a difficult test of will or strength. Or helping your fellow folk in their troubles. When you have found one, please write it down on your paper. And when we say together, “Lugh, accept our offering,” place your offering in the fire.

 

Allow a few moments for the folk to write.
