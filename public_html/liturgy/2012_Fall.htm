Welsh Fall Equinox Ritual

 

Written by Mike Talvola from original rite by Coedwig

Some collective changes

For Raven’s Cry Grove, ADF public rite September 22, 2012

 

 

INITIATING THE RITE

 

Mike: Brothers and sisters!  Welcome to the Alban Elfed ritual of Raven’s Cry Grove, ADF!  We stand here at the threshold.  Warrior, call a guardian for our rite.

 

Heather: [standing in the North]

 

“Lleu Llaw Gyffes [thloo thlaw geef-ffis]  , Lleu [thlou] of the deft arm. With your strength and skill you were named. With your cunning, you were armed. With the magic of your great uncle, you were wed. Brother of Dylan, foster son of Gwydion.  We ask you to come into the grove and protect it and those within as we do our work this evening. We offer you ale [ale offering] , so that you do not thirst, and bread, [bread offering]  so that you do not hunger as you keep your vigil.”

 

“Derbyn ein offrwm!  [der-been ayn hof-room]  Accept our offering!”

 

“Bydded Felly.”  [buth-ed veck-lee]

 

 

Mike: Here we create our threshold.  We open the roadway to the sacred center.  Warrior, mark this space that the outdwellers shall not enter.

 

Warrior draws a line at the entrance.

 

Heather: Lleu Llaw Gyffes [thloo thlaw geef-ffis]  - we ask you to watch this threshold.

 

 

Mike: Brothers and sisters, as we prepare to make offering to the Outdwellers, please avert your eyes so as not to form a relationship with them, and also look to the Outdwellers in your own hearts.  This is a celebration of love.  All that we carry into the nemeton today will flower.  Take only that which you wish to root, grow, and flourish.

 

Warrior!  Make offering and appease the Outdwellers who do not support our ways.

 

Heather:  Spirits of this place and afar. Those who do not follow our ways, but follow another way. You, who were known to our gods as the Corannyeid [Cor-an-nee-ayd], we respect the differences we share and ask that you neither hinder the energies we raise this night nor prevent us from honoring the Kindreds and receiving their blessings.

 

Take these spirits [tequila offering] as a gift from us, and by doing so acknowledge our request and leave us in peace for this rite.

 

“Bydded Felly”   [buth-ed veck-lee]

 

 

Meg: Brothers and sisters, release the cares of your mundane life.  Take a deep breath in and allow your mind to be still.  Thoughts outside of this time are released.  You are here spiraling ever forward in the present.

 

Feel the pull of our sacred space, calling you, whispering on the wind.  In this space we create our sacred realities.  We can become all the Kindreds believe us to be.  Purification allows us to transform into our sacred selves.  Please come forward, be purified, and attune yourself to mythic time.  Let go and cross the divide.

 

 

Form the procession. 

 

Procession- “Come we Now as a People”


Folk take their place in the circle. 

 

When all folk are in place,

 

Meg:  By the light of the fire and the might of the water, make this Grove be whole and holy.

 

Druids cense and asperse around the circle.

 

Purification Chant

Fire in the Heart, fire of the mind

Fire on the wind, fire out of time

 

Fire of the night, fire of the soul

Fire pure and bright, fire make us whole.

(Repeats until finished)

 

Mike: ring chime 3x3 times saying:

Ancestors! (chime 3x)

Nature Spirits! (chime 3x)

Shining Ones! (chime 3x)

 

We have crossed the threshold into sacred space.  Please reconnect with the sacred cosmos as we prepare to open the gates to the otherworlds, the place where the kindreds will reveal themselves once more.

 

 

 

 

HONORING THE EARTH MOTHER

 

Meg: “ Let us begin by honoring the Earth, our home.”

 

Kaila:  " Spirit of this world. You who are the ground we walk on, build our homes on and sleep on. You who are the sea bordering us, the mountains surrounding us, the sky above us. You are everything a mother should be. We give you our love, honor and respect, Goddess that is this world. May the sky not fall, the sea not rise, and the land hold firm.

 

[grain offering]

 

Derbyn ein Offrwm!  [der-been ayn hof-room] Earth Mother, accept our offering!”

 

 

Statement Of Purpose

 

Meg: Today we gather between the Earth and Sky.

To worship and honor the Kindred as one people.

To sing their praise and make our sacrifice.

As we seek their blessings in return.

 

Mike: 

Croeso y Pob Bendith [croy-so uh pob ben-dith]. Welcome and many blessings. We are here to celebrate the day of Alban Elfed, the Light of Autumn. In the days of our ancestors, this day would signal the end of the first harvest. They would rejoice and give prayers of thanks to our gods for all the blessings they had received. Now, in remembrance of those days, we too give due honor to the powers for all they have assisted us with this year.

 

This day is also the Equinox, when day and night, light and dark are balanced and present in the world in equal force. It is only right that we honor those forces on this evening. Tonight we will call on Beli Mawr and Don; the father and mother of the House of Light. And we will call on Llyr [Thleer] and Penarddun [Pen-ar-thun], the father and mother of the House of Darkness.

 

Without them, there would be no balance. Without them, we would be at the mercy of the forces of chaos. They are the fathers and mothers of the gods. Let us honor them this afternoon.”

 

Meg: As our ancestors did and our children may do, so do we now: we are here to

honor the Old Bargain between mortals and the Kindreds.  So let us join together as one folk to make our offerings in joy and reverence.

 

“Bydded Felly”   [buth-ed veck-lee]

All: “Bydded Felly”   [buth-ed veck-lee]!

 

 

Inspiration  

 

Meg: “ And now let us call for inspiration, so that we may speak the rite in beauty”

 

Mike:  “ Ceridwen, changer of shape, bane of Gwion, mother of Taliesin; Come into our grove now with your magic cauldron, the source of awen, the fiery waters of inspiration. Be here with us and fill our heads with your magic so that our words ring sweet in the ears of the powers. Make our voices strong, our minds clear, our bodies able. Guide our rite in the way of truth.

 

[red wine or red flower petals with oil offering]

 

Bydded Felly!”   [buth-ed veck-lee]

 

 

 

 

 

Establish the Group Mind

 

Jackie: [Two powers meditation]

 

 

Recreate the Cosmos

 

Meg: Brothers and sisters, let us now re-establish the Sacred Center of the Worlds.

 

 

The Three Realms

 

Mike:

The waters support and surround us.

The land extends about us.

The sky stretches above us.

And the center burns a living flame.

Let us pray with a good fire.

May all the Kindred bless us.

May our worship be true.

May our actions be just.

May our love be pure.

Blessings and honor and worship to the holy ones.

 

 

The Hallows

 

Meg: “ Let us sanctify the hallows by which we make offering.”

 

Well

Mike:  “ Ffynnon Sanctaidd [Sank-tayth],, Sacred well. Waters of Annwfn [An nov’n]. Portal to the worlds below. Source of all. Well of dark wisdom. Blood of the earth. We make our call with bright silver [silver offering] and ask that you be in this vessel and in us.

 

Ffynnon Sanctaidd, llifa o’n mewn [thlowss-ka own me-oon]! Sacred Well, flow within us!”

 

 

Fire

Heather: “ Dan Sanctaidd [Sank-tayth], Sacred Fire. Holy light of the shining ones. Embodiment of inspiration. Fire in the head. Element of controlled chaos. You are capable of both creating and destroying. Bringing both life and death. We make our call to you with sweet smelling oil [oil offering]  and ask that you be in this vessel and in us.

 

Dan Sanctaidd [Sank-tayth], llosga o’n mewn [thlowss-ka own me-oon]! Sacred Fire, burn within us!”

 

Tree

Jackie:  “ Coeden Sanctaidd [Coy-den Sank-tayth], Sacred Tree. Boundary of all worlds. Creature rooted deep and crowned high. Sacred Pillar. Carry us with you to your depths and raise us up to your heights. Be for us the center of the grove, the hub of the wheel, connecting us to all the worlds. We make our call to you with sacred water and sacred smoke and ask that you be the center of this place.  [insense and water offering]

 

Coeden Sanctaidd [Coy-den Sank-tayth], tyfa o’n mewn! [tuv-a own me-oon]  Sacred Tree, grow within us.”

 



Opening the Gates

 

Mike: “ We have sanctified the hallows. Now let us call on the Gate Keeper to bring the worlds together.”

 

Meg:  “ Gwyn ap Nudd [Gwin ap Neath], Lord of the Otherworld. Out of the deep unknown, we call you. Lord of Annwfn [An nov’n]. Out of the obscure mist, we call you. Ruler of the Tylwyth Teg [Til-with Teg], the fair folk, out of the dark woods we call you. You who lead the dead to your court under the hill during your nightly rides across the sky, we call on you to make the way ready for us. Come to us, with your blackened face and dapple-gray horse, with your ghostly hounds trailing behind you. Ward us, so that we walk the ways in safety.

 

Derbyn ein offrwm! [der-been ayn hof-room]  Accept our offering and hear our call!  [triskele offering]

 

Share your magic with ours as we open the ways.

Let us, together, make the well a gate to the worlds below!

Let us, together, make the fire a gate to the worlds above!

Let us, together, make the tree the crossroads of all the worlds!

 

Agorer Y Pyrth [a-gor-er uh pirth]! Let the Gates be opened!

 

 

 

 

 

INVITING THE THREE KINDREDS

 

Honoring and Invoking the Powers

 

Mike: Brothers and sisters! So that we may honor them, make now our offerings to the Kindreds – the Ancient Ones, the Noble Ones, and the Shining Ones.

 

Ancestors

Kaila: Hynafiaid [Hee-na-fee-eyed], Ancestors. Those whom we are related to in blood and those that we revere for their gifts to the world. It is because you lived that we live. Great warriors, priests, chieftains, and laborers of the distant past. Grandparents, parents, aunts, uncles, and dear ones of the recent past. You who are the Mighty Ones, we call on you as our kin. Take this simple offering and join us in our rite. 

 

[whiskey offering]

 

Hynafiaid [Hee-na-fee-eyed],  derbyn ein offrwm! [der-been ayn hof-room] Ancestors, accept our offering!

 

Meg: Brothers and sisters, close your eyes, take a moment, and feel the Ancestors as they join us here.

 

Allow a few moments

 

 

 

Nature Spirits

Russell: Ysbrydion [Ees-bree-dee-own]  Natur, Nature Spirits. Spirits with whom we share this world. The animals of fir, fin, feather, and scale. Of rock, leaf, flower, and branch. Tylwyth Teg, fair folk, and faery kin. You who are the Noble Ones, we call on you as our allies. Take this simple offering and join us in our rite. 

 

[fruit offering]

 

Ysbrydion [Ees-bree-dee-own]  Natur, derbyn ein offrwm! [der-been ayn hof-room] Nature Spirits, accept our offering!

 

Meg: Brothers and sisters, close your eyes, take a moment, and feel the Nature Spirits as they join us here.

 

Allow a few moments

 

 

 

Deities

Meg: Dduwiau [Thoo-ee-aye], Our gods and goddesses. Those of the House of light, the House of Darkness, and the patrons of all present here. You are the eldest and the wisest. You are our greatest of guides, best of friends, and most nurturing of parents. Where would we be without the graces of the gods? You who are the Shining Ones, we call on you as our elders. Take this simple offering and join us in our rite.

 

[oil offering]

 

Dduwiau [Thoo-ee-aye], derbyn ein offrwm! [der-been ayn hof-room] Shining Ones, accept our offering!

 

Meg:  Brothers and sisters, close your eyes, take a moment, and feel the Gods and Goddesses as they join us here.

 

Allow a few moments

 

 

Praise Offerings

 

Mike: Children of the earth, as the spirit moves you, come forward and make praise offerings to the Kindreds or your Patrons.  You may offer at the well, fire, or tree, sing songs, recite poetry, dance, or do whatever would please the Kindreds.

 

Folk make offerings as they will

 

 

KEY OFFERINGS

 

Main Invocation

 

Mike: “ This is the last high day before the beginning of the New Year. The last high day before the time of darkness begins again. Before the cycle begins again. A time of balance. This is the time to give thanks for this year’s cycle, and for this year’s gifts. So let us now call on the Patron Powers of this rite so that they may hear our prayers.

 

Let us begin by calling on Beli Mawr and Don, the father and mother of the children of Light.”

 

Heather:  Beli Mawr. Hear us now, Bright One. Fire god. God of light. You are the fire of the year. At Beltaine you are the Fire of Life; at Samhain you are the Fire of Death. At this time of the harvest, burn brightly in our hearts and be for us the Fire of Bounty! Cast your flashing, fiery eyes upon those that call to you now and enter our place of worship. Great god, crowned with fire, join us in our rite!

 

[frankincense offering]

 

 

Kaila: Don, mother goddess, earth goddess. Hear us now, source womb of all life! You have existed from eternity and exist still as the background principle of all realities. You are ruler of the earth and the waters under the earth that spring up as the rivers, lakes, and sacred wells. Great goddess cloaked in a mantle of stars, be for us the substance of growth and bounty and join us in our rite!

[milk offering]

 

 

Mike: “ Let us now call on Llyr [thleer] and Penarddun [Pen-ar-thun],  the father and mother of the children of Darkness”

 

Mike: Llyr [thleer]. God of the sea. God of the dark waters. Hear us, Lord of the kingdoms under the waves. You are the ruler of the perilous seas that surround our lands.

Your oceans bring life and death to those that explore its surfaces and depths. They are the waters of chaos. At this time of the harvest, be for us the waters of abundance. Great god of the sea, whose eyes are like that of an eagle’s, join us in our rite!

[stout offering]

 

 

Meg: Penarddun [Pen-ar-thun] . Hear us now, daughter of the earth goddess and fire god, wife of the sea god. You are born of the light and married to the darkness. Both aspects of light and dark reside within you. You are the High Goddess, goddess of the headlands. At this time, when light and darkness are of equal length and strength, be for us the principal of Balance. Great goddess, standing proud, join us in our rite!

[kahlua and milk offering]

 

 

Main Offering

 

Mike: “ As this day symbolizes the end of the harvest, we will offer up grain as we did at the beginning of the harvest. But we will not offer just simple grain. We will imbue it with our own reflections of this year’s bounty and with our prayers of thanks. We will pass this loaf of bread around so that all may lay their hands upon it. Think of this past year. Think of the joys and sorrows. Of your successes and failures. Be grateful for the struggles that have shaped you into your present form. Be grateful for the experiences that have allowed you to grow.  You may express your gratitude either spoken or in silence.

 

As you do this think fondly of this rite’s patrons, the representatives of Earth, Sea, and Sky - of light and dark - below, above, and middle. They are the balance in our lives…

 

(Bread is passed around.  Soft background music here)

 

Beli, Don, Llyr [thleer], Penarddun [Pen-ar-thun]! Your children have poured their hearts into this offering! May you find the energy contained within to be your nourishment and strength, much as we are strengthened and nourished by your grace!

 

Great Powers… Derbyn ein haberth [der-been ayn haw-berth]. Accept our sacrifice!

Bydded Felly!    [buth-ed veck-lee]

 

 

Bardic Offering

“Calan Lan”

RECEIVING THE BLESSINGS

 

Mike:

Brothers and sisters, all our offerings, all we have done so far in our rite, have led to this moment.  This is magic time!  We have made offerings to the Powers, and now we shall ask for their blessings in return.  Visualize the blessings that each of you desire and need from the Kindreds.  See it clearly in your mind’s eye. And when we ask for the Waters of Life, when we say, “Give us the Waters”, see the blessing you desire descend into the upraised cup like a mist, filling the cup.  And when we drink of the Waters, you will receive your blessing.  So close your eyes and take a moment to visualize your needs, and the needs of our Grove, that we may all receive the blessings we desire.

 

Allow time for the people to meditate on needs and desires.  Meg pours mead into the cup and druids hold it.

Dduwiau, rhowch i ni'r Dyfroedd [Thoo-ee-aye, row-ook ih near Duff-royth]! Shining Ones, give us the waters!

Thanking

Meg:  Shining Ones. Gods and goddesses of all those gathered here. Diolch i chi, [di-olch ee hee]we thank you!

Closing the gates

 

Meg:  Gwyn ap Nudd, Lord of the world beyond. Lord of the Tylwyth Teg. For aiding us in keeping the ways open during our rite, we say to you, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Meg:  Once again, we have continued in our traditions, honoring the Gods,

Ancestors and Nature Spirits, just as in times of old.  We have made offerings to our Kindreds and received their blessings in return.

 

But great energy has been raised within us this day!  Let us be grounded once more.
