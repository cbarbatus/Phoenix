Statement Of Purpose

 

Paul: Today we gather between the Earth and Sky.

To worship and honor the Kindred as one people.

To sing their praise and make our sacrifice.

As we seek their blessings in return.

 

Heather:  Beltaine is the fertility festival par excellence! This is indeed an

extravagant holiday. At this time of year, roses are in full bloom, peaches and berries are ripe. This is the Love Dance of the Gods, the Wedding of Heaven and Earth, the Bridal feast of the Goddess! At Beltaine, our energies are turned toward the Love and Union that brings a Harvest of new life among us.

 

Paul: As our ancestors did and our children may do, so do we now: we are here to

honor the Old Bargain between mortals and the Kindreds.  So let us join together as one folk to make our offerings in joy and reverence.

KEY OFFERINGS

 

 

 

MAY QUEEN & LIGHTING OF THE FIRE

 

A woman in a dark cloak emerges, and stands to the north of the Hallows, with her head bowed and her cloak closed up around her.

 

Paul:  At this time, let the Fire be made dark and all the Land be returned to the will of Belenos /BELL-le-knowss/, Lord of the Sacred Flame.

 

The fire is extinguished

 

Paul: Let there be among us, seven men who will offer to the Lord of the Fire to kindle the flame for summer, if it should be his will.

 

7 men are given 7 pieces of wood

 

Paul:  Mighty Belenos /BELL-le-knowss/, let this wood and these servants be worthy in your eyes. (faces south) Otherworld kin, you who have gathered here at this southern flame, grant us the Summer’s light! Go raibh maith agat /guh ruh MAW huh-gut/ Belenos. Let the Fire of Summer be lit!

 

All: Let the Fire of Summer be lit!

 

The wood is laid beside the second fire, which is relit from the flame in the South.

 

Heather:   Light once again returns to the land, and once again we seek to join Her and follow in her ways. Through Winter, she gives us of herself as An Chailleach /uh XAL-yuhx/, who tests our will and gives us strength. It is now that the Fire of Summer will burn away the guise of the Crone!

 

In elder days, Erin the goddess was the mother of clans, called Aine /AWN-ya/, queen of the noble ones. Aine the mighty mated with mighty men and from them she bore many peoples. As each of them grew old she would renew her youth and love again.

 

So for us is the living earth goddess, who grows old with the winter and renews her youth in this merry, magical time. With each of renewing she brings the flowing of new life, the erotic blossoming of every kindred, not just for the continuing of clans, but for the delight and regeneration of all beings.

OMEN

 

Paul: Seer, what blessings and wisdom do the Kindreds offer us in return?

 

Russell: takes the omen

 

Paul:

Druids, all our offerings, all we have done so far in our rite, have led to this moment.  This is magic time!  We have made offerings to the Powers, and now we shall ask for their blessings in return.  Visualize the blessings that each of you desire and need from the Kindreds.  See it clearly in your mind’s eye. And when we ask for the Waters of Life, when we say, “Give us the Waters of Life”, see the blessing you desire descend into the upraised cup like a mist, filling the cup.  And when we drink of the Waters, you will receive your blessing.  So close your eyes and take a moment to visualize your needs, and the needs of our Grove, that we may all receive the blessings we desire.

 

Allow time for the people to meditate on needs and desires.  Paul pours May Wine into the cup and holds it.

 

Heather: O Ancient, Noble and Shining Ones!  We have offered to you, so we ask for your gifts in return.

 

Paul elevates the Waters.

 

Hallow these waters!  Bless our lives with Magic, Power and Abundance as we drink of your essence.  Kindreds, we ask you, give us the waters of life!

Heather: Behold the waters of life!  Druids, drink of the waters of life!
