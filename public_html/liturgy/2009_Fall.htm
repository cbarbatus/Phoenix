Opening Statement

 

PD:  Welcome and many blessings. We gather today to celebrate the day of Alban Elfed, the Autumnal Equinox.

Light and shadow exist in harmonious balance. The wheel of the year now turns towards colder days and the approaching Winter.  We begin the holding time before the New Year at Samhain. 

At Alban Elfed our ancestors celebrated the end of the first harvest.  They gave thanks for the bounty and fruits of their hard work.  They would feast together as community and share their plenty together, as a ritual of thanks to the Gods, the land, and each other.  Witch’s Thanksgiving grew out of this tradition.

In honor and remembrance of the old ways, we also gather to give thanks to the Gods, thanks for our harvests, and to each other in community.

Statement of Purpose

 

PD:  We come together as a community today to celebrate the Fall Equinox.  We come to honor the first of the last harvest. We will praise the Kindred and personal Patrons.  We will offer to our ritual Goddess, Rhiannon, the Great Queen of Sovereignty.  Honoring the harvests each of us has sown, we will each have an opportunity to lay our hands on grains of the Earth to give thanks. We will also share our energies in a special magical working, where we can leave behind that which needs to be shed, as we walk forward to the year to come.

Magical Working

 

[Warriors will set up balance beam as PD explains]

 

PD:  At the Autumn Equinox, the light of the sun, and the darkness of night are in equal balance. 

We can look at the wheel of the year at this half way point where day and night are equal. 

Let’s utilize this opportunity to walk in balance at this hinge point.  By walking in balance along the Balance Beam, we can leave behind an aspect of ourselves that we need to let go.  What do you leave behind in the light half of the year?  Self doubt, sadness, perhaps jealousy…these are just ideas.  The winter is approaching and we all need to have our full strength to get through those long dark nights. 

 

Please take notice to really think about what you want to leave behind.  Really mean what you say.  Words are very powerful. Please be 100% ready to actually leave behind what you state.

 

As you cross, you will be asked by one of our Elders, “What do you leave behind”

You will answer with one word, “I leave behind…”

 

[Elder 1 and Elder 2 will stand on each side of balance beam each taking a turn asking and helping the participant cross; PD will wait for the participant to cross]

 

[Single drummer plays heartbeat]

 

As celebrant comes down PD will respond, “Move forward.  You leave it behind you; you leave it in the past”
