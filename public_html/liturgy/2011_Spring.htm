PURIFICATION

Participants are to wash their hands in a bowl of khernip (lustral) water containing a small amount of salt as they enter and are censed.  They are anointed with water on the forehead by a Grove member with an additional bowl of lustral water saying “You may enter in peace”. (Kristine and Tami)

 

As is tradition, the items for ritual are carried in by the folk.  First in, a young girl carries flowers followed by the PDs and then the rest follow in.  A basket of barley with a knife buried inside which will later be used to cut the bread to offer Hestia.

Opening Statement

 

PD1:  Khairtete oloi kai Kalos Irthete Adelphia!  Greetings and welcome brothers and sisters!  We come together to celebrate the return of life on Earth, we celebrate the festival of The Cholaia, the verdant green, the returning of the Korē to her mother Queen Demeter. 

 

These many cold and rainy months Demeter has mourned and searched for her daughter.  She has searched the ends of the Earth in pursuit of finding her beloved Korē, She has pleaded with mortals and gods alike to aid her in her search.  Yet no solace can she find.  She is consumed and exhausted from her quest.

 

Demeter’s cries of sorrow have reached the realm of the Underworld where her daughter Persephone is called Queen.  Upon hearing Her mother’s grief, she knows it is time to leave Her dark realm…She feels her mother’s lament in her soul.  She knows her time has come once more to attend to Her Mother and to life on Earth.  She removes her crown, she drinks of the River of Forgetfulness… and so begins her journey back again to the land of the living.  

 

As She walks green shoots come into sight from deep beneath.  Tender blossoms are ready to burst with color.  We can feel the Earth warming to our touch.  Anxious are the grains to rise up and greet the sun once more as we are anxious for warmth and light after the cold, dark winter.

(Kaila)

O Goddess, Earth, of Gods and mortals the source,
Endued with fertile, all destroying force;
All-parent, bounding, whose prolific powers,
Produce a store of beauteous fruits and flowers,
All-various maid, the eternal world's strong base

Immortal, blessed, crowned with every grace;
From whose wide womb, as from an endless root,
Fruits, many-formed, mature and grateful shoot.
Deep bosomed, blessed, pleased with grassy plains,
Sweet to the smell,and with prolific rains.

With rapid whirl, eternal and divine,
Whose frames with matchless skill and wisdom shine.
Come, blessed Goddess, listen to our prayer

And make increase of fruits thy constant care;
With fertile Seasons in thy train, draw near,
