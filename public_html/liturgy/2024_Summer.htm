KEY OFFERINGS

 

Elizabeth: Thargelia was a celebration of the birth of Artemis and Apollo. The twin gods born to Leto. It was said Hera, jealous again of her wandering husband, cursed Leto to find no solid ground to on which to give birth to her children. When Leto went into labor she searched far and wide over every inch of the Greek Islands. No one would give her refuge for fear of Hera's anger. Zeus realized something must be done and raised an island, Delos, from the sea, because it was so new it was not yet anchored to the ocean floor and Hera's curse did not apply.

 

Her labor with Artemis the first born was painless and easy. But Hera, still angry, kidnapped Eileithayia, the goddess of childbirth, preventing an easy labor for the second child, Apollo. Leto labored for nine whole days to deliver Apollo and Artemis was the one to help her mother deliver her twin brother. In thanks for granting her a place to labor Leto attached Delos to the ocean floor with four anchor points.

 

Thargelia was celebrated in two parts the first by purifying the people and town using a scapegoat or two. The second part was to offer the first fruits of the crops to the gods, particularly Apollo. This was both as a gesture of thankfulness and also to curry his favor so that he looks kindly on the rest of the summer and does not ruin the remaining harvest with excessive heat, or even pestilence.

You are the protector of children and childbirth and earned that upon helping your mothers labor when you were only newly born yourself.
