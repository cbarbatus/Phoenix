KEY OFFERINGS

THE SYNOIKIA
Susan: The Synoikia [Zu-noy-KEE-ah] festival in Ancient Greece was a time of unification. It was a time when representatives of every kinship in Athens would gather and make sacrifices of ewes and bulls on behalf of their extended family groups – or phratries [FRAY-trees] as they were called - to Zeu Phratrios [ZEH-oo FRAY-tree-ohs] (in his aspect of Patron of Brotherhood). Once they had honored Zeu Phratrios [ZEH-oo FRAY-tree-ohs] on behalf of their kinships, the groups then came together to honor Athena, the patroness of their city. They gave her thanks for her continued protection against outside threats and made vows to set aside those things that had caused animosity between them, for the sake of a united Athens. For they recognized that it was being united from within that aided Athena’s ability to protect them from the opposition without. 
In later times, an offering to Eirene [AI-ree-nee] (the embodiment of Peace) was then also made, for the sake of Athens’ future.
This is not Athens, but you don’t need to be told how factional our own city is… our state…our country. You were each given a small bull figure. Today, we will celebrate the Synoikia [Zu-noy-KEE-ah]  by first thinking about the various kinships we are members of in our lives – families, organizations, companies, this grove – any group of people that you identify with as a member.
Allow a few moments for the folk to consider this.

O much-honored, Zeus, supremely great, to you our holy rites we consecrate.
We pray to you, king divine, for all things ‘round your exalted head shine.
The earth is yours: the mountains high, the sea profound, and all beneath the sky.
Magnanimous, commanding, sceptered Zeus; the All-parent, whose almighty power shakes earth loose;
Even Nature trembles at your mighty nod; loud-sounding, armed with lightning, you are a thundering God.
Source of abundance, purifying king, O various-formed, from whom all natures spring;
Benevolent Zeus, hear our prayer - grant peace divine, necessary wealth and blameless health to all in your care. (adapted from Orphic Hymn #24 to Zeus)
