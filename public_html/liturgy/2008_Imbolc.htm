Imbolc  2008

Adapted from Imbolc 2007 and updated by Raven’s Cry Grove Members

 

I. Establishing the Grove

 

PD Begins with chime 3x3

 

Call for Protector

D:  Great Dagda, king and champion of the Tuatha De Danann, Mighty One, God of the Druids, who carries the club of life and death and the cauldron of plenty, we ask that you join us here and protect this sacred place and time and those present as we honor your bright daughter this night.  We offer you your favorite drink and grain to sustain you as you keep your vigil here. (Offering of whiskey and oats).

An Dagda, accept our offering!  “Biodh sé Amhlaidh”  (All repeat)

Procession – We Approach the Sacred Grove

 

D1 and D2:

Cense and Asperse as people enter.

 

D3: (Brideog doll and a candle is carried by a woman leading the procession and placed on the altar). 

 

PD: We Welcome you Brid, Radiant Lady, who warms our hearths and lives. (hearth fire and altar candle is lit from the candle in procession while those present sing).

- Fire in the Heart


Opening Statement
PD: As our Ancestors have done, so we, too, gather here today to honor A Brid, Goddess of Divine Inspiration which gives us purpose in life, Goddess of the Fires of Healing, Goddess of the Hearth and the Forge, Protector of Women and Children.  Today, we celebrate the Feast of Imbolc as we sense the spark of the much-needed renewal that is taking place within us and within the earth.  The seeds that have been planted within us or under our feet, are moving and growing, beginning to spring forth, and push their way to the surface.   The return of the sun also heralds in this time of hope and needed activity.  So let us join here today together as one to make our offerings to Brid, our Ancestors, the Sidhe, and the Gods, and may our children continue this tradition in the generations to come.  “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

Earth Mother

PD: We will begin by honoring the Earth Mother.

D:

 

Earth Mother, Mother of this Land,

You whose womb has given life to all and you whose body sustains it –

Whose waters flow within us from the vast oceans to the tiniest streams,

Whose fires inspire us as they dance and lick the air,

You, whose trees, with mighty branches, reach for the sky with open arms with their strong roots, plunged deep into your heart and to the realms below, bridging to us all   

   the worlds. 

 

We honor you this night for you have nurtured us as only a mother could.

And we ask that you bless us with your presence in this rite so that we may know a greater connection to you and to all your children here.

 

Earth Mother! glacigí n-iobairt seo! (gloch-i-zhjee, nah-bey-RUT-shuh)

Accept our sacrifice!  (Gives offering of flower seeds).

 

 “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”


Inspiration

PD: We will now call for the fires of inspiration, so that our words may be spoken in beauty and eloquence.

D: Oghma, eloquent “Sunny-faced” champion of the great bards and the Tuatha De Dannan, kin of Brid and The Dagdha, shining protector of your people, join us here, and spark the flames of inspiration within our hearts.  Bright Oghma, guide our songs, words, and offerings of praise this night, and add your beauty to this rite.

(Offer hazelnut oil)

 

“Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

 

II. Opening of the Grove

 

Grove Attunement- D: Two powers centering

 

The Hallows

PD: We now sanctify the hallows by which we make offering.

 

Well

D1: (Kneel over the well as if to speak to into the well)

Sacred threshold of the worlds below.

Where the primordial waters ebb and flow.

Where the land of the dead in which we find,

(Lifts water from the well with their dominant hand and allows it to run out back into the well)

Is reflected in us,

within the depths of our minds.

(Stands and charges the well with the wet hand)

We now make the well a portal,

in this place,

So that all gathered here may bathe,

in sacred time and sacred space.

(The offering is made and is reverently observed as it “reaches the underworld”

 uisci naomh (ish-kuh neev) (an offering of silver spiral is made to the well) Sacred Well, flow within us!  “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

 

Fire

D2: Lucent One, we honor you, tine naomh (tinneh neev) (an offering of oil is made to the fire) Sacred Fire, burn within us!  “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

 

Tree

D3: Crann Domhanda, World Tree
From your dead, fallen leaves, new life springs forth.
You give us oxygen.
You heal us with your leaves, flowers, resins, and roots
Cailleach's Hand comes out from your trunk in the Earth from which you stand

I give you back my breath
and these offerings in deepest gratitude.

(give offerings of incense, water and sacred oil)


Join us in our rite this evening, with your healing strength and age old wisdom.

 

Great holder of the threshold, we honor you, Crann Naomh Fas Ionain!

Sacred Tree, grow within us!  “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

Opening the Gates

 “ We have sanctified the hallows for our worship. Now let us call on the Gate Keeper Mannanan Mac Lir, to aid us in opening the portals as we stand between the worlds.

 

D:

 

Osclaítear na geataí anois(OS-kluh-TEER na GAH-thay anish)!  Let the gates be open!

 

III. Offering to the Powers

Honoring and Invoking the Powers
PD: “ Let us now call on the kindreds so we may honor them.”

Ancestors

D1:

A Shinseara, blessed Ancestors, we call that you might join our celebration and receive due offering.

Come to the Gates, honored ones; hear our call, we your children who remember.

We offer you our worship, our reverence and our love.

 

To our grandmothers and grandfathers, our beloved dead, blood-kin and heart-kin; ancient tribes of our blood, we bid you welcome.

To the elder wise ones who guide their people, wise women and men of ancient days, we bid you welcome.

To those whose bones lie in this land, whose hearts are tied to it, whose memory holds it; ancient tribes of this place, we bid you welcome.

 

We hear your whispered voices speaking words of wisdom into our unconscious minds.

Your whispers awaken our dreams, our hearts, our desires.

You who once walked upon the earth, you who are part of our shared life eternal,

we praise you with all that is sacred in our lives.

 

A Shinseara! In great respect, we offer to you! (whiskey to the well)

Join us, and accept our sacrifice! ALL: (Repeat)

 

“Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

 

 

Nature Spirits

D2: Nature Spirits, A sprideanna na talaimh (uh SPRid-jun-na na TALL-iv),

    *

A sprideanna na talaimn, spirits of the land:

 

Across the land you gallop and race.

Beneath the land you burrow and crawl.

Through the air, from place to place,

By feather & wing you climb and fall.

Through the waters, you cavort & swim

In mirky river or ocean blue...

 

All creatures of day and evening dim,

In Brighid's name, we offer to you.

 

Nature spirits, accept our sacrifice!

 

(make offering)

 “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

 

Deities

D3:

A Dheithe! ( ah-yay-hah)

 

Oh great gods and goddesses, and creators of all that is, and all that will be.

 

Mothers and fathers of heroes and heroines,

both tragic and glorious.  Those who sustain us here.

 

You who bring order to chaos, who bring rain,

whose heat and light shines down on the earth,

giving it light and warmth so life can exist,

you, whose forests provide needed oxygen for our eco-system

so life can exist.

 

You who are the life force, the manifestation of all.

 

We, your children, wish to honor you this night.

Join us in this rite, and accept this offering.

(give offering of scented oil)

 

Oh great and Shining Ones! Join us and accept our sacrifice!

“Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

Personal Offerings

PD:  If anyone is called to make offering to their gods and allies, please do so now.

 

 

INVOCATION TO BRIGID:

PD:  We now call upon the Great Lady of this Season, The Dearest Lady of the Emerald Isle, A Brid.

 

PD: A Brid, bright, virtuous lady, daughter of the Daghda, great woman of the arts, endless fire of inspiration, and the seed’s primordial burst, dearest protector of women and children, strong lady of the magic smithy, paragon of metalwork, poetic prophecy, and life-craft, the sweetness of blackberries and cream and the cow nurturing its calf.

Walk among us in your stately, milk-white gown

Your steps, the notes of the anvil’s song,

Your healing felt in the depths of warm hearts.

Join us here so that we may craft honor before you and among your people.

 

 

 A Brid, glacigí n-iobairt seo! Accept these offerings.  Failte raibth. Welcome! (offers a bowl of blackberries and cream to the hearth fire.)

  

Main Offering to Brigid

-A Song and commitment to be offered.  

 

PD:  Announces the focus of the offering for the occasion.

Candle passed:  I will bring beauty, I will create, I will heal, I will protect, I will inspire, I will bring magic, I will offer service…etc.

 

May this flame of Brigid that holds our intent keep our way bright and our commitment firm… 

 

 

 

 

 

D: (Anvil is struck after each statement, with a final statement and striking of the anvil three times).

 

The Omen

PD: “ We have honored the powers and given to them. It is now time for the flow to turn and the powers to reciprocate. Let us begin by asking what blessings and messages the powers wish to impart.

Blessing Cup

PD: We are now ready to receive all the blessings the powers wish to give us and we may also ask for a boon from them at this time. (Druid pours mead into the cup.)

D1: Shining Ones, Ancestors, Nature Spirits, we, your children, have offered to you here.  Dear Ones, in the spirit of ancient honor and hospitality we ask you, Give us the waters of life!

 

All:  Give us the Waters of Life!

D2:  We seek your blessings, of wisdom, of magic, and of rebirth!  Give us the waters of life!

 

All:  Give us the waters of life!

D3:  We seek your blessings, of prosperity, of plenty, of abundance and of love!
Kindreds, we ask, Give us the waters of life!

All: Give us the waters of life!

 

PD:  (Takes the Cup and says) These are the Waters of Life.

SONG – Pour the Waters –

 

(After the song, the cup is passed around)

Thanking

PD:  We have given to the powers and the powers have given to us. Let us now thank them before they depart.

D:  A Brid (ah-VREEJ), you who we have honored here tonight, Goddess of the flame within all things! Go raigh maith agat (Guh- ruh MAH a-GAT), we thank you!

 

D:  To those gods and allies who those present called upon, we say, Go raigh maith agat (Guh- ruh MAH a-GAT), we thank you!


D: A Dheithe, Shining Ones! For your many blessings, we say Go raigh maith agaibh (Guh- ruh MAH  a-GIV), we thank you!

 

D: A sprideanna na talaimh, spirits of the land! Go raigh maith agaibh (Guh- ruh MAH a-GIV), we thank

you!

 

D: A shinseara, mighty ancestors! For your guidance and presence we say, Go raigh maith agaibh (Guh- ruh MAH a-GIV), we thank you!

D: Oghma, eloquent one, we say, Go raigh maith agat (Guh- ruh MAH a-GAT ), we thank you!

 

D: Dagda, Eochaid Ollathair (YO-keed, ahllah-TAY-ir), protector of our Grove this night! Go raigh maith agat (Guh- ruh MAH a-GAT ), we thank you!

D: Great Earth Mother, all that nurtures and surrounds us! Go raigh maith agat, (Guh- ruh MAH a-GAT) we thank you!  We offer to you dear Mother all the blessings left unused.  May you drink deep, we thank you!

 

 

Closing the Gates

PD:  We now call upon our gatekeeper once more to thank him, and to close the portals of this place, for this time.

 

D: Mannanan Mac Lir, keeper of gates, by your magic and ours we end what we began. We thank you for guarding us and guiding us!
Now let the fire be flame
Let the well be water;
And let the tree be but the very heart of us all that we carry forth from this place!
Let all be as it was before. Let the Gates now be closed!

 

ALL: Let the Gates now be closed!


Grounding - (D)

PD: Let us end as we began. (Chime 3x3)

Brothers and Sisters, the rite has ended, let us remember this time of fellowship and go in peace, renewed in our commitments.
