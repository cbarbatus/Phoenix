Introduction:

 

PD1:  The Children of the Earth are gathered in this Sacred Grove to keep the rites of Lughnassadh.  As our ancestors have done before us, we gather together in peace to share in the success of the Harvest.  We gather so that we may celebrate the outcome of our year-long relationship between the Tribe and the Land, and so that we may continue to be mindful of that delicate relationship in the year to come.

 

     We honor here today, the Shining young hero, Lugh, champion of Ireland; we remember his foster mother Tailtiu who gave her life to feed her people by clearing the plains.  For Lugh created this festival in her honor.  Now, the tribes of the Land meet here in the rivers of our blood and the landscapes of our bodies to share and play in peace, to honor Tailtiu and the Shining Ones, to show feats of skill, and to partake of the fruits of the season.  We gather here connected to all time in that same spirit of peace, camaraderie, remembrance, and joy.  Let there be peace in all the land and peace in our personal and collective growth, and may future generations gather together to celebrate their harvests in peace and plenty.

Short Statement of Ritual Purpose

      Now is the beginning of the Ripe Time, when the labor of the people brings the Land to bear, when the Power of the Sun gives itself to the Power of the Earth.  We gather here for rest and pleasure, before the labor and lessons of reaping and gathering that we have sown takes place.   May we always plant our seeds in honor.

 

      We are here to honor Lugh, known by many names, for his light shines strong in many lands –  where great ancient cities are named for him.  So strong is his presence throughout time that his story, bright gifts, and sacrifices are found in many cultures throughout the world.   In days of old, the bounty of the land and its connection to the virtues and greatness of its King was well known by all.  Lugh represents this ideal King, for he brings the skill of all arts to bless his people, he strengthens his people and becomes their sacred center, and he remains ever mindful of his relationship with the Land and the Sovereign Goddesses who grant life and bounty.

If the Sun does not die for a time, the green earth turns to grey.

If the Sun does not die for a time, red burns to black.

If the Sun does not die for a time, the earth and all will be barren dry!

 

So the God’s man mated with a woman of the Giants, in magic and courage.  Their union brought forth the Child of Light and Shadow, Lugh, whose name means light.  Fostered in the Isle of Apples, he learned every skill and became Master of all Arts.

 

How like a spear is the ray of dawning!

How like the dawn is the rising light of victory!

How like victory is the joy of wisdom!
