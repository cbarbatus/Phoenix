- Purification of Participants as they enter the Nemeton

 

People are censed and aspersed as they enter the area.  As is tradition, the items for ritual are carried in by the folk.  First in, a young person carries a decorated olive branch followed by the PD and then the rest follow in.

 

The folk place the items near the altar and proceed to two lustral bowls (khernips - containing a small amount of salt and the waters of Delphi) to wash their hands and dry them.  As they each wash their hands they should say:

 

 “I remove the last of all that is profane.  With Athena’s blessings, I stand clean and true in this sacred time and place.” 

 

For this rite especially it is important that great care be taken in the purification process as much as possible.  A sign at each bowl with what to say would be helpful.  Celebrants will wash hands first and arrange items quickly on the altar while the folk wash their hands.

 

A basket of barley (with a knife buried inside- which will later be used to cut the bread) will be passed around so that each person can take a handful of the barley which they will throw upon cue to purify the altar and the sacred designated space.

 

 

- Opening Statement and Precedence

 

     PD:  We belong to the gods and the gods belong to us, so let us pray with a good fire.  Let us offer with a full heart, knowing that when we do, we nurture our connection to them and support the very gods themselves.  In remembrance, we strengthen their powers.  In honor, we balance the worlds.  In love, we bring peace and keep the Old Ways alive and present. 

 

PD:  May our worship be true!     All:  May our worship be true!

 

PD:  And with the aid of Athene, may we mark this space as sacred!

 

All:  Jenito!  (All throw the barley toward the altar)

 

     Brothers and sisters, welcome to this Nemeton and welcome to this celebration of the Summer Solstice, the time when the sun and moon stand in equal power as the earth begins to yield up its first fruits of the harvest, where the old year of vegetation falls away to allow for a new harvest and new blessings.  In the spirit of this season and the ancient Greek festival of Thargelia, we will call upon Artemis, goddess of purification, prudent huntress of the fruit-producing moon. We will also call upon her brother, Apollon, the golden god of the skies, fields, and seasons, who drives away pestilence, and who advances summer and winter in alternate dance.  We have purified ourselves, this space, and this land to make ready for the gods and their blessings, to purge the old year, and to make ready for the new harvest that will open before us.

Female and Male with borrowed rays you shine,

Now full-orbed, and now tending to decline.
Mother of ages, fruit-producing Moon, whose amber orb makes Night's reflected noon:
Lover of deer, splendid, queen of Night, all-seeing power, bedecked with starry light.
