Setting Historical Precedence – Statement

 

 

PD: Salvete Citizens and guests!  We gather today to honor the gods of Ancient Rome who live with us and provide for us.  Our rite today is not purely Roman, rather an ADF Druid rite, for as was the way of Ancient Rome, rites of groups other than those of the city, Rome, are performed per their own customs.

 

The festival of Liberalia was held in honor of Liber Pater, Liber the Father, a fertility god whose worship dated from near 500 B.C..  Liber Pater, along with his female equivalent and partner Libera and the earth goddess Ceres, shared a temple on the Aventine Hill in Rome.  Liberalia, it is said, was celebrated with sacrifices, crude songs, processions, and liberties of all sorts.

 

As we have gathered in this sacred space, let us now center and prepare ourselves for this rite and this work.

    *  Wash both hands in clean water and pray:

        May this water cast out all impurities from my substance as from lead to gold.

    * Place both hands upon your head and pray:

        Purify my mind.

    * Bring the arms down to your sides with hands in gesture to your body and pray:

        Purify my body.

    * Place both hands on the chest, over the heart and pray:

        Purify my heart.

    * Take a moment to focus and become fully present and affirm:

        It is so.

Statement of Ritual Purpose

 

[Senior Druid]:  We are here to honor the gods.  So, let us pray with a good fire.  Let us offer with a full heart knowing that when we do, we nurture our relationship with the Kindreds.  In remembering, we strengthen and sustain the gods themselves.  In honor, we balance the worlds, and in love we bring peace and keep the Old Ways alive and present.

 

PD:    Here today we honor the gods of Ancient Rome with whom we share existence each day.  We reassure them that, though millennia have passed, they are not forgotten.  We pay special homage today to the triad who were revealed in 496 BC by the Sibylline Books to be worshipped and thereby end a terrible famine.   To bring fertility, enjoyment, and abundance today, we honor Liber Pater, Libera, and Ceres.
