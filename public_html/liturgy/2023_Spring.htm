INITIATING THE RITE
 
PROCESSION AND PURIFICATION
 
LISA: Druids!  Welcome to our Jare Gody /YAR-eh GO-dee/, a Polish spring ritual for Raven’s Cry Grove, ADF!
 
Release the cares of your mundane life.  Take a deep breath in and allow your mind to be still.  Thoughts outside of this time are released.  You are here spiraling ever forward in the present.
 
MIKE: Feel the pull of our sacred space, calling you, whispering on the wind.  In this space we create our sacred realities.  We can become all the Kindreds believe us to be.  Purification allows us to transform into our sacred selves.  Please come forward, be purified, and attune yourself to mythic time.  Let go and cross the divide.
 
LISA: By the light of the fire and might of the water, may this grove be made whole and holy.
 
Form the Procession: At least two druids form an arch with some kind of long stem flower (roses, sunflowers, etc.)  Everyone walks under and is censed as they exit the arch.

KEY OFFERINGS

LISA: Let me introduce you to Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh], the god of spring.

MIKE:  Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] was the son of the mighty god Perun [puh-ROON], the king of the sky, the mighty god of lightning and thunder.  Perun [puh-ROON]’s sworn enemy, his brother Veles, the god of the underworld, kidnapped the baby Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] and raised him in the underworld.  The Polish underworld is a place of eternal wet, fertile Earth filled with lush greenery filled with eternal life and abundance. 

LISA:  Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] was raised as a son by Veles.  Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] kept his foster father’s livestock without fail until the curiosity of budding adulthood called him to the upperworld, the land of his true father, Perun [puh-ROON].

 

MIKE:  When Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] returned to the land, the end of darkness and death came with him.  When Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] undertook his sacred walkabout to the upperworlds, a sacred journey called the hoditi, Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] brought about a sacred rebirth to the land, a roditi.  The back of winter was broken as Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh]’s hodit caused a roditi bringing spring to the land.

LISA:  Yet the cycle of life and death continues.  Yarilo [Yuh-RILL-oh] returns to his foster father’s lands to keep his livestock, returning the land to winter and death, once the cycle of life has spent her turn of the wheel. 

MIKE:  And thus the sacred balance between the upper and underworld remains unbroken. 


LISA:

Polish Spring Tradition

Effigy of Morana
 
MIKE:  Another Polish tradition relating to the death of winter relates to the tale of the goddess Moranna. 

LISA:  She is the embodiment of winter, who must be burned away with the fire of the returning sun or drowned in the flowing waters of the melting slow.  When the wheel turns to darkness again, she returns to rule until the time for her effigy returns again.
