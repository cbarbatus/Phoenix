I. Establishing the Grove

 

PD Begins with chime 3x3

 

Call for Protector (Shelby)

D1:  Great Dagda, king and champion of the Tuatha De Danann, Mighty One, God of the Druids, who carries the club of life and death and the cauldron of plenty, we ask that you join us here and protect this sacred place and time and those present as we honor your bright daughter this night.  We offer you your favorite drink and grain to sustain you as you keep your vigil here. (Offering of whiskey and oats).

An Dagda, accept our offering!  “Biodh sé Amhlaidh”  (All repeat)

Procession – We Approach the Sacred Grove

(Brideog doll and a candle is carried by a woman leading the procession and placed on the altar). 

 

PD: We Welcome you Brid, Radiant Lady, who warms our hearths and lives. (hearth fire and altar candle is lit from the candle in procession while those present sing).

- Fire in the Heart

Opening Statement
PD: As our Ancestors have done, so we, too, gather here today to honor A Brid, Goddess of Divine Inspiration which gives us purpose in life, Goddess of the Fires of Healing, Goddess of the Hearth and the Forge, Protector of Women and Children.  Today, we celebrate the Feast of Imbolc as we sense the spark of the much-needed renewal that is taking place within us and within the earth.  The seeds, that have been planted within us or under our feet, are moving and growing, beginning to spring forth, pushing their way to the surface.   The return of the sun also heralds in this time of hope and needed activity.  So let us join here today together as one to make our offerings to Brid, our Ancestors, the Sidhe, and the Gods, and may our children continue this tradition in the generations to come.

 

 

Earth Mother (by Kaila)

 

PD: We will begin by honoring the Earth Mother.
 

Danu, Earth Mother, beloved keeper of the waters of heaven…mysterious Mother,

Both Sun and Moon, keeper of all light and darkness.

Beautiful one, you shift like the currents in a river.

Within your womb and from your sinuous, snake body we are borne to this Earth.

 

We your children, arriving naked on your glittering shores,

long still for the watery home within you from which we passed from death to life….

In you…..through you.

 

Danu, generous ever flowing stream of Celestial Grace, keeper of the waters of Heaven and creator of all, we call to you tonight.

 

You who have so lovingly brought this child from the brink of darkness, who brought her back into light, back to your nurturing and warm embrace;

 

You who have borne all life from your watery depths and have created brilliant order from numbing chaos, favor us with your nurturing and transformative presence and be with us tonight as we honor you and bear you praise.

 

Hear your children, Oh Mother of all creation, as we offer this to you!

Beloved Mother of all creation, hear your children!

We bring you this offering in reverence and love to honor you this night.     

 

DANU – EARTH MOTHER – JOIN US HERE AND ACCEPT OUR OFFERING!

Bíodh sé Amhlaidh!  All: Repeat.

 

 

Inspiration

PD: We will now call for the fires of inspiration, so that our words may be spoken in beauty and eloquence.

Oghma, eloquent “Sunny-faced” champion of the great bards and the Tuatha De Dannan, kin of Brid and The Dagdha, shining protector of your people, join us here, and spark the flames of inspiration within our hearts.  Bright Oghma, guide our songs, words, and offerings of praise this night, and add your beauty to this rite.

 

“Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

Well (by Sarah)

 

Uisci naomh! (ish-kuh neev)

 

Oceans. Rivers. Sky-Sweat. Morning Dew. I call to you.

Please discover your home this Imbolc night, here, in our grove, in our Well.

Spiral depths of the deep, cool waters, gather here,

Reflect the moon in your mirrored surface.

Allow us to gaze in that mirror,

So we may see ourselves in you and speak to your worlds.

Sacred Well, mysterious waters, be sanctified,

May we bathe in your healing and wisdom.

 

Bountiful waters, flow within us.  All:  Repeat.

 (an offering of silver is made to the well)  Bíodh sé Amhlaidh!

III. Offering to the Powers

Honoring and Invoking the Powers
PD: “ Let us now call on the kindreds so we may honor them.”

 

Ancestors  (by Mike) 

 
A Shinseara!  Ancestors!



We, your children who remember, call to you!



To the ancient tribes whose legacy flows in our veins,

To the elder wise ones of ancient days who guide the people,

To those ancient ones whose bones lie in this land,



We hear your whispered voices speaking words of wisdom into

our unconscious minds.  Your whispers awaken our dreams, our hearts,

our desires!  You who once walked on this earth, you who are

part of our shared life eternal, we praise you with all that is

sacred in our lives.



A Shinseara!  Mighty Ancestors!  Be here now at the gates!  In great respect,

we offer to you!  [whiskey to the well]



Join us, and accept our sacrifice!

Biodh sé Amhlaidh



Nature Spirits  (by Meg)

 

Beloved Nature Spirits

In all your many forms,

The babbling brook,

The birds who sing,

The tiny spirits within all things,

The rocks, the fae, the whispering breeze,

The furred, the finned, and of course, the trees…

We cherish your presence in each day

And ask you now to come this way.

We bring you offering and invite you to join us in our rite this night.

Nature Spirits, We Welcome You!

Accept our sacrifice!  “Bíodh sé Amhlaidh”

 

Deities (by Melissa)

 

A Dheithe (uh YAY-huh), Shining ones,
You whose light shines upon us even in the darkest night
You whose great wisdom leads us but also allows us to walk alone in strength,
You know no boundaries, no time, no place, it is only possible with you.

We ask that you join us here this night and share with us your light and wisdom.
May partake of you and share it with those around us this evening
Bring us closer to the sacred and bless us with your sweet potential.

Illumined Ones join us here for we wish to do you honor. We offer this in devotion.

Accept our sacrifice! Bíodh sé Amhlaidh.  ALL: Repeat!

INVOCATION TO BRIGID: (by Lisa)

PD:  We now call upon the Great Lady of this Season, The Dearest Lady of the Emerald Isle, A Brid.

 

PD: A Brid, bright, virtuous lady, daughter of the Daghda, great woman of the arts, endless fire of inspiration, and the seed’s primordial burst, dearest protector of women and children, strong lady of the magic smithy, paragon of metalwork, poetic prophecy, and life-craft, the sweetness of blackberries and cream and the cow nurturing its calf.

Walk among us in your stately, milk-white gown

Your steps, the notes of the anvil’s song,

Your healing, felt in the depths of warm hearts.

Join us here so that we may craft honor before you and among your people.
