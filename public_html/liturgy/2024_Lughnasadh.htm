Introduction

Lead:  The Children of the Earth are gathered in this Sacred Grove to keep the rites of Lughnasadh.  As our ancestors have done before us, we gather together in fellowship to share in the success of the First Harvest.  We gather so that we may celebrate and reflect upon the outcomes of our personal harvests which mirror our relationship between the Tribe and the Land.  

Co-Lead:  We honor here today, the hero, Lugh, champion of Ireland, who embodies the perfection of this relationship.  As is custom, we also honor his foster mother, Tailtiu /TAL-choo/, who gave her life to feed her people by clearing the plains and plowing the soil.  Lughnasadh was a festival where the different tribes and their chieftains gathered at the center of Ireland to feast and to compete in games of skill and honor their connection in Sovereignty of the Land. We meet today to sustain brotherhood and celebrate the harvest in peace and plenty.

Statement of Purpose

Lead: Now is the beginning of the harvest season, when the labor of the people brings the Land to bare, and the Power of the Sun gives itself to the Power of the Earth. We gather here for rest and pleasure before the labor and lessons of reaping what we have sown take place. May we find that we have sown our seeds well.

It is fitting that we honor the god of light and grain, Lugh, who is known by many names. In the days of olde in Ireland, a basic truth was well known by all - that the virtue and generosity of the High King was directly connected to the bounty of the land and the wellbeing of its people. To the degree that the king was honorable, the land flourished, but if the King was corrupt and selfish, the land would cease to bare its gifts and the people would suffer.

Co-Lead: Lugh became this ideal King, for he brought the skill of all arts to bless and strengthen his people, his virtue made the land blossom, and he became the people's sacred center, ever mindful of maintaining a relationship with the Goddesses of the Land and of Sovereignty.

Lugh is also for us in this season, the bright spear of sunlight who leads us through this dark time of the year and the dark stages of our transitions and transformations. He is the paradox of the shadow-time as well the welcoming ray of the light of dawn.
