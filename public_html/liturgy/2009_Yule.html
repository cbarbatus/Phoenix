Public Mid-Winter Ritual – January 2, 2010

By Z.L. Bennett


I. Preliminary Work

- Chime 3x3

- Purification of Space and calling on a Protector.


Protector

D1: Golden Lugh, of the strong and long arm, honorable child of shadow and light, Bright-Bladed Steed, of sure speech and steady spear, we, your children, ask that you join us here and lend your protection here this day. (offering of ale to the North, outside the circle to the warrior area, signified by a spear or sword). Samildanach, we offer this to you with respect and gratitude. Accept our sacrifice!

All: Accept our sacrifice.

-Processional – We Approach the Sacred Grove

(Folk are aspersed and censed as they enter the Nemeton, they will walk in a circle until all are present)


- Opening Statement and Precedence:

PD: Dear Pagan Folk, We gather in this place to share circle with each other, to honor the old year as it passes and to enhance our relationship with the Queens of Bounty and Abundance by offering our love, our thanks, and our commitment to sustain our relationship with them in this bright new year.


We also will work collectively here to ensure that our community, our pagan brothers and sisters, and those of like mind, who have been suffering during these economic times, find strength and new inspiration to lead them toward financial stability, safety, and well-being.


We belong to the gods and the gods belong to us, so let us pray with a good fire. Let us offer with a true and full heart, knowing that when we do, we sustain our relationship with the Shining Ones. In honor, we strengthen them, In peace, we balance the worlds, and in love, we keep the Old Ways alive and present. As our ancestors have done before us, so we do now, and may our children come to do so long after we are gone.


Powers of Inspiration –

PD: We begin our work this night by calling on the powers of inspiration, that we may speak here in truth and beauty.


D1: A Brid, Lady of Inspiration who manifests the divine to us in this world through vision, shaping, and speech. Join us here and guide us now in beauty. May our words and actions be sweet and true to the gods and the spirits all around us. (Offer oil to the anvil placed on a rock (which has three spirals painted on it) in the center and then hit three times.) Brigid, Accept our offering. ALL: Brigid, accept our offering.


II. Opening of the Grove

PD: We will now prepare ourselves by becoming the axis and establishing the magical elements to create our grove for our work.


-Grove Attunement – Two Powers and the mists


- Attuning with the Airts and/or Land, Sea, and Sky


PD: May there be peace in the East,

May there be peace in the South,

May there be peace in the West,

May there be peace in the North,

May there be peace in the Center, the axis of all Worlds, and the Threshold of Myth and

Magic. (Standing in center wand, spear, or staff is pointed toward each direction).


PD: And may there be peace in Land, Sea, and Sky, in Body, Mind, and Spirit. (With wand, spear, or staff a triskel is drawn overhead in the center of the Nemeton with the following words).

Let there be peace in the known (1st spiral of triskel drawn),

The unknown (2nd spiral of triskel drawn),

The realms that are far beyond our reach (3rd spiral of triskel drawn),

And those that are all around us (a large circle deosil drawn above).

*Inspired and taken in part from work of Ian Corrigan.



- Honoring the Hallows (Well, Fire. and Tree)

PD: Let us now sanctify the hallows from which we make offering.


(Three Druids come forward and go to their chosen hallow. They will remain until all hallows are honored)


D1: Cauldron of the Underworld and Chthonic Ones, Portal to the Ancestors, Dark churning waters of mystery, chaos, and wisdom, we sanctify you here with reverence and offer this to you. (offer silver to well)


D2: Fire of the Heavens and Shining Ones, Portal to the Gods, Bright tongue of inspiration, order, and will, we sanctify you here with reverence and offer this to you.

(offer oil to fire)


D3: Ancient World Tree, of the Mid-Realms and the Sidhe, Portal to all the worlds, Sturdy keeper of the Land, of history and time, we sanctify you here with reverence and offer this to you. (offer smaller painted stone to tree)

D1: (Still standing at hallow) Sacred Well, flow deep within us! All: (Repeat).

D2: (Still standing at hallow) Sacred Fire, burn bright within us! All: (Repeat).

D3: (Still standing at hallow) Sacred Tree, grow strong within us! All: (Repeat).



- Opening of the Gates

(This can be done another way by an experienced Gate Opener.)

PD: Now that we have prepared ourselves and this place, let us open the portals for our work. We call upon our Gatekeeper, Mhannain Mac Lir.


D: Masterful Prince, ride your gilded chariot, and come gently to us.

Piercing the elegant blue of seductive, turquoise seas

Deep in the dappled mist of ancient spiraled threes,

Where the breath holds still, in the journey.

Move toward your seekers here and open the mystery,

Where moonlight shines from islands of silvern apple trees.

Fine-formed one, ward our steps, part with ease the boundaries’ way,

Where all worlds flow as one, and let us now, together, open the gates.



D: Gatekeeper, Accept now this gift of silver (offers silver triskel to the well) and

Let our words sound in the depths. (speaker uses hands or wand to direct

energy to each hallow as…a grove sacrificer adds oil to the well)

Let our offerings rise in the fire.

(a grove sacrificer adds oil to the fire)

Let the devotion in our hearts be carried to all the worlds.

(a grove sacrificer puts oil on bile)

(Toning or movement, could be done here to build energy and accentuate the opening in a strong way).


A Mhannain Mac Lir, Osclaitear na Geatai anois.

Let the Gates be Open!


ALL: Let the Gates be Open! (Hold with brief pause so that the energies can be felt more

acutely by the attendees).


III. Offering to the Powers


- Honoring the Kindreds (Ancestors, Nature Spirits, Shining Ones – each spoken from the heart. Each should do the following: 1) Evoke images for the folk; 2) honor/praise deity; 3) Ask them for their presence here; 4) Give offering (all while keeping as brief as possible :o).


Ancestors: ... End with: Join us at our rite and accept our sacrifice.

Nature Spirits ... Join us at our rite and accept our sacrifice.

Shining Ones ... Join us at our rite and accept our sacrifice.


- Praise offerings of the folk


- Calling on the Deities of Occasion: Banba, Fohla, Eriu – and the Mother of this Land


PD: We call upon the Triple Three who bestow Sovereignty and abundance, wed to the light of inspiration, the waters of wisdom, and the Land’s bounty, the great goddesses of the Land of Ireland and sisters of the Great Earth Mother of all Lands.


D1:

Banba, you are both the waters of chaos and the land that would first emerge from those pulsating seas. Queen of mountains, lakes, and islands, Lady of Munster magic, wed to MacCuill, wisdom’s son, Your children remember and honor you here. Join us at our rite and accept our offering.

(Offer to the well, 3 hazel nuts).


D2:

Fotla, you are the furrow, fertile and ready, wed to MacCecht, plowshare’s son. Queen of the harvest time, vine-ripe, potent, and strong, Lady of Bounty, Boon, and Blessing, Your children remember and honor you here. Join us at our rite and accept our offering. (Offer to the tree, a combination of herb, fruit, and flower).


D3:

Eriu, You are the high ship, the lofty green, an incantation most cunning. You grant land to the generations of all time. It is you for whom the land of Ireland is named and it is you to whom Amairgen kept his promise. Regal, Stately One, who secures balance and judges rightly, wed to MacGréne, the Golden Son. Your children remember and honor you here, and we wish to do you honor. Join us at our rite and accept our offering. (Offer to the fire three draughts of scented oil).


PD: Mighty Queens and Dear Sisters Three, Hail and Welcome.

We now call to One like you, a sister, the Great Mother of this Land, of this place.


D4:Mother of this Land, our ancestors traveled here from many lands and you sustained them here, with your rich, fine body and myriads of treasures. You sustain us still and whisper close in the tall grasses that embrace in your cool, sweet breath. Queen of this place, You, who laugh in coyote songs, thrash in quakes and storms, survey on hawk pinions, and dance to native drums, we, your children remember you, and we wish to do you honor. Join us at our rite and accept our offering. (Offering of blue corn (3 pieces each) to each of the three hallows).


PD: Queen of this Land and Place, Hail and Welcome. - Mothers, Sisters All, Hail and Welcome!


- Main Offering:


PD: We know that when we are blessed with much, the doors open for us to share more with others. We only need look around to see the blight on pagan professions and pagan-owned businesses, and we know from our ancient history as well as modern history that when financial times are hard, scapegoats are found and religious fervor usurps the worth of new ideas, humanitarianism, and the individual. Tonight, Let us forge a commitment of action here with the Great Mother who nurtures us, so that by our actions, we can come to accept greater abundance in our lives in grace and generosity. Let us ask here too, that our brothers and sisters find inspiration, hope, and palpable changes to benefit and secure their futures.


As Pagans and lovers of nature, we remember that truly all abundance comes from the Earth Mother and all that she encompasses. We are called by our earth-based traditions to live in balance with Her. But how much more do we ask than offer? Today, in your presence great Goddesses of the Land, Mothers and Grandmothers all, we will offer up as a community our commitment and love to the you, as the gods and mortals have done for thousands of years.


Now, connected in all time, take a flower, and as you hold it send to it your love and commitment – the one thing that you know you can do to serve the Mother this year. When you are ready please bring it forward and place it in the basket in the center here. Remember to choose your commitment with care and mix it with a lot of love. (place on center of altar table if indoor rite).


When all flowers are ready…


Now, we will amplify our offerings exponentially by charging these flowers together, Send the Great Mother all you can for her to use as she needs.

(Charging as a group is done over the offerings – raising through from Earth Mother Chant to toning charge)


Earth Mother Chant:

Earth Mother we honor your body

Earth Mother we honor your bones

Earth Mother we sing to your spirit

Earth Mother we sing to your stones.


PD: Dear Mother of us All, hear your children, know our love, and accept our offering.

(PD - Offer flowers to the fire).


-Taking of the Omen

PD: Before receiving our blessings from the Kindred, let us first still ourselves to hear the message the Kindred wish to impart to us.


IV. The Blessing

- Calling for Blessing

PD: The flow has now turned and now it is the happy time when we received the blessings of the Kindred into each of our lives. We begin first by asking for the waters of life.


D: Sovereign Ladies and a Dheithe, we have offered here to you and have made commitment to care for and protect the land. In the way of the boons of ancient hospitality we say. Shining Ones Grant us the waters.


All: Shining Ones Grant us the Waters.


D: A sprideanna na talaimh, we have honored you this day, seeking for you a peace between us, seeking for you a happy land in which to dwell. In offering you our alliance we say, Spirits of Nature Grant us the Waters.


All: Spirits of Nature, Grant us the Waters


D: A Shinseara, we have remembered you here and stand as your blood and bone in honor in this life and in this realm. To further our connections and traditions we say, Ancestors, grant us the waters.


All: Ancestors Grant us the Waters.


PD: I ask, brothers and sisters do you accept these blessings?


On behalf of the community standing here, I ask for the blessings of the Mothers and the Land. May the businesses of your children, that serve you dear Mother, thrive. May we have Land to care for and protect, may we have abundance to do good in the world, and may we come to realize the building of temples on pagan Land so that we may come together to worship and build community for the long term, in safety and in peace. Finally, on behalf of the community here, I ask that those around the world who are in greatest need come to receive it sweetly from our dear Mother’s hands. Brothers and sisters do you ask for this blessing with me? (Response).


Then drink heartily – for these are the waters of life!!!


Pour The Waters – sung twice


After the Waters are passed … Pour remaining


Here in this cauldron lie pieces of brick, the mixture of fire and water, earth and air, shaped by human hands in order to build. I now pour the Blessings of the gods onto these bricks and charge them to imbue these blessings so that each of you may carry these chards as a symbol of laying the foundation for the future, of placing the first brick into a means of service to the Land and our people. By taking one, you are accepting prosperity and abundance and the responsibility that implies. You are also confirming your earlier commitment to The Great Mother, as one that will be done.

(Bricks are taken)


Let us stand for a moment in silence as each of you holds your blessings and charged dreams. Contemplate and visualize the foundation you build, and the foundation that each of us standing here together builds.


V: Closing and Ending


- Affirmation of Success:

PD: Happily we have offered to the Great Mothers and happily we have received their blessings which will serve the Land and the people of the Old Ways.


- Thanking the Powers: We now thank the powers that have aided us in our rite:

Mother of this Land, and Banba, Fotla, and Eriu

Kindred

Powers of inspiration

Protection

Great Mother of All – (offering back what is left of the blessing to the earth (flowers and the cauldron – hole is dug already outside the complex and the brick and flowers are buried there.


- Thanking Gatekeeper and Closing the Gates

PD: We now call upon our Gatekeeper once more.


D: A Mhannain Mac Lir, Lord of the mists through which we travel, for holding the gates open with us and keeping vigil over our steps, Go raibh maith agat, we thank you.

Now, Ageless One, we ask that you work your will with ours so that we may close the gates while allowing this sacred time to linger here as we walk in the Realms of the Kindred.

Let the well now be, the water that sustains us.

Let the fire now be, the flame that warms our nights.

Let the tree now be our shelter and comfort when we gather under its leafy boughs.


A Mhannnain Mac Lir, Dúntar na Geataí anois! Let the Gates Be Closed! ALL: Repeat.


- Regrounding and Centering


Recessional - Final Blessing and announcement of ending.:

PD: Thank all of you here for joining us here in sacred time… and as we prepare to leave this sacred time, let us sing together once more.


- Recessional – Walk with Wisdom by Sable

Walk with wisdom from this hallowed place

Walk not in sorrow, our roots shall ere embrace.

May strength be your brother, and honor be your friend,

And luck be your lover, until we meet again.

We will sing once and then holding hands sing chorus once more walking in a circle clockwise!


- Closing /Musical Signal (chime 3x3)


D: And so, Brothers and Sisters as we began so let us end (chimes 3x3). Our rite is at its end, let us go forth renewed in fellowship and hope, ready to be people of plenty in order to be people that model and offer hope and peace.
