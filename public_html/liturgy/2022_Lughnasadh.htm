Introduction

Paul:  The Children of the Earth are gathered in this Sacred Grove to keep the rites of Lughnasadh.  As our ancestors have done before us, we gather together in fellowship to share in the success of the Harvest.  We gather so that we may celebrate and reflect upon the outcomes of our personal harvests which mirror our relationship between the Tribe and the Land.   

Chelsea:  We honor here today, the young hero, Lugh, champion of Ireland, who embodies the perfection of this relationship.  As is custom, we also honor his foster mother, Tailtiu /TAL-choo/, who gave her life to feed her people by clearing the plains and ploughing the soil.  Lughnasadh was a festival where the different tribes and their chieftains gathered at the center of Ireland to feast and to compete in games of skill, while never drawing a sword from its sheath in anger or retribution for past misdeeds.  These tribes came to also honor the central connection of the Land to Sovereignty.  Here, do we meet today, representing our tribes in the rivers of our blood and the landscapes of our bodies to share and play, to honor the Kindred, to nurture peace and unity, and to partake of the fruits of the season.  We gather here connected to all time in that same spirit of camaraderie, respect, and joy.  Let this rite sustain brotherhood in all the land and accord in our personal and collective growth, and may future generations gather together to remember and to celebrate their harvests in peace and plenty as well.

Statement of Purpose

Paul: Now is the beginning of the Ripe Time, when the labor of the people brings the Land to bear, when the Power of the Sun gives itself to the Power of the Earth. We gather here for rest and pleasure before the labor and lessons, of reaping what we have sown, take place. As we plan to gather in our store for the fallow time to come, may we find that we have sown our seeds well.

In this time of the harvest then, it is fitting that we honor the god of light and grain, Lugh, who is known by many names, for his light shines strong in many lands and many cities are named for him. In the days of olde in Ireland, a basic truth was well known by all - that the virtue and generosity of the High King was directly connected to the bounty of the land and the wellbeing of its people. To the degree that the king was honorable, the land flourished, but if the King was corrupt and selfish, the land, over time, would cease to bear its gifts and the people would suffer.

Chelsea:     Lugh became this ideal King, for he brought the skill of all arts to bless and strengthen his people, his virtue made the land blossom, and he became the people’s sacred center, ever mindful of maintaining right relationship with the Goddesses of the Land and of Sovereignty, those who, alone, grant true power and bounty. 

Lugh is also for us in this season, the bright spear of sunlight who leads us through this dark time of the year and the dark stages of our transitions and transformations. He is the paradox of the shadow-time as well the welcoming ray of the light of dawn.

Lisa: To the great Tailtiu /TAL-choo/, the Mother of All the Earth, and all Powers that have aided our rite, filled with gratitude, we return to you all blessings we cannot carry here. Uphold us now in the world as you have in our rite as we say once more
Go raigh maith agat!  /guh ruh MAW huh-gut/. We thank you!

(Remaining libation is poured into the earth.)

ALL: We thank you!


Closing the Rite

Paul: As we started, so let us end.

3 X 3 chimes are sounded

Chelsea: May the strength of Lugh be with us, may His beauty delight us, may His talents be upon us. May he drive away the storms from this grove, the community gathered here, and from all who honor the earth.
And now, once again, our Bards pay homage to the evening’s festivities.

Bardic Farewell

Paul: Brothers and sisters, let us feast in peace and blessings. The rite is ended.
