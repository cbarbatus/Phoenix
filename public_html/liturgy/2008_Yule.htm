Mid-Winter Rite (Welsh)                                                                  written by Coedwig

- For the Bards                                                                                  adapted by Z. Lisa

                                                                                                      

I. Establishing the Grove

 

PD Begins with chime 3x3

 

Call for Protector

“Lleu Llaw Gyffes, Lleu of the deft arm. With your strength and skill you were named. With your cunning, you were armed. With the magic of your great uncle, you were wed. Brother of Dylan, foster son of Gwydion. We ask you to come into the grove and protect it and those within as we do our work this evening. We offer you ale [ale offering], so that you do not thirst, and bread, [bread offering]so that you do not hunger as you keep your vigil.”

“Derbyn ein hoffrwm! Accept our offering!”  “Bydded Felly.”

 

Procession- “Mothers and Fathers of Old” -   Censing and Aspersing done here also.

 

Opening Statement- PD

 

     Welcome, brothers and sisters, and many blessings to you in the New Year. We have come together to honor the powers that sustain us, especially the bards, those who harness the fires of the head and heart to create beauty, myth, and prophecy, in order to make the divine more palpable in this world.  As myth and history reveals, the ancient bards of Wales, were considered the finest of all.  So tonight we will honor Taliesin, the paragon of all the bards of Wales.   We will also have the opportunity to honor those makers of story, poetry, and song who have inspired our lives as well.

 

     As those who have gone before us has done, let us celebrate the turning of the year in the company of community, gathered around a good fire in the cold of winter to weave tale and song, stirring the embers of the fires of the head to inspire the work of our hands in the promise of the New Year.  Let us celebrate tonight with love and warmth in our hearts.  May this time of reflection and time spent together here, fan the sparks within us that will herald our new passions and new growth for the year.

 

Earth Mother

 

PD: We must always begin by honoring the Great Mother of All.

 

" O beloved Mother, Great spirit of the earth, our home. You who are the sea bordering us, the mountains surrounding us, the sky above us, and the knowing in the heart that beats in us . It is from you that all life as we know it springs forth. Tonight, we call on you as Don, Mother Goddess of Wales and ask that you watch over us this night and bless this rite. We give you this offering of grain with so much love and gratitude in our hearts.  Goddess Don, Earth Mother, derbyn ein hoffrwm, accept our offering!”

 

Inspiration

 

PD:  And now we call for inspiration, so that we may speak the rite in beauty.

 

Ceridwen, keeper of the cauldron, amen, source of awen, the fiery waters of inspiration. Changer of shape, bane of Gwion, mother of Taliesin; Come into our grove now and kindle our heads with the brightness of your inspiration so that our words ring sweet in the ears of the powers. Make our voices strong, our minds clear, our bodies able. Guide our rite in the way of truth.

 

Bydded Felly!

 

II. Opening of the Grove

 

Grove Attunement- Two powers centering

 

The Hallows

 

PD: We now sanctify the hallows by which we make offering.

 

Well

May the waters of the earth be in this vessel; the rivers, streams, lakes, oceans and the waters of darkness that flow under the earth. May the dark realms of the beloved ancestors and chthonic gods and spirits be brought here and channeled through this vessel. We make our call with bright silver so that these waters may be a shrine for our honoring of the ancestors.

 

Ffynnon Sanctaidd, llifa o’n mewn! Sacred Well, flow within us!”

 

 

Fire

May the sacred fires kindled by our ancestors be remembered this night. May this flame be for us the fires of the hearth, of love, passion, and inspiration; the bright heat of the sun and the cool light of the stars and moon, and the bright places where the shining and celestial gods and spirits live. May all those things be brought here and channeled through this flame. We make our call with sweet smelling oil so that this flame may be a shrine for our praising of the gods .

 

Dan Sanctaidd, llosga o’n mewn! Sacred Fire, burn within us!

 

Tree

May this stave of wood be for us a tree. The physical representation of the connectedness of the world. And through its branches reaching high and its roots digging deep may we also span the heights and depths. May the green places where the nature gods and goddesses and nature spirits reside be brought here through this sacred tree. We give it water so that it may drink deep, and incense so that it may breath sweetly. May it be now a shrine for our worshiping of the spirits of all the worlds.

Coeden Sanctaidd, tyfa o’n mewn! Sacred Tree, grow within us.

 

Opening the Gates

 

PD: We have sanctified the hallows for our worship. Now let us call on the Gate Keeper to bring the worlds together.

 

Gwyn ap Nudd, Lord of the Otherworld. Out of the deep unknown, we call you. Lord of Annwfn. Out of the obscure mist, we call you. Ruler of the Tylwyth Teg, the fair folk, out of the dark woods we call you. You who lead the dead to your court under the hill during your nightly rides across the sky, we call on you to make the way ready for us. Come to us, with your blackened face and dapple-gray horse, with your ghostly hounds trailing behind you. Ward us, so that we walk the ways in safety.

 

Derbyn ein hoffrwm! Accept our offering and hear our call!

 

Share your magic with ours as we open the ways.

Let us, together, make the well a gate to the homes of the ancestors!

Let us, together, make the fire a gate to the homes of the gods!

Let us, together, make the tree a gateway to all the spirits of the Earth!

 

Agorer Y Pyrth! Let the Gates be opened!

 

III. Offering to the Powers

 

Honoring and Invoking the Powers

 

PD: Let us now call on the kindreds so we may invite them here and honor them.

 

Nature Spirits

Ysbrydion Natur, Nature Spirits. Spirits who share this world with us.

Children of the green earth. Animals of fir, fin, feather, and scale. Creatures of leaf, flower, rock, and bark, The Tylwyth Teg and faery of all lands. On this night we give you our respect and ask that you join us at our hearthside. Noble Ones, we give you this offering of fruits and say together:  Nature Spirits, accept our offering!  Bydded Felly!

 

Ancestors

Hynafiaid, Ancestors. Those whom we are related to in blood and those that we revere for their gifts to the world. Great warriors, priests, chieftains, bards, and hand workers of the distant past. Grandparents, parents, aunts, uncles, and dear ones of the recent past. On this night we give you our love and ask that you join us at our hearthside. Mighty Ones, we give you this offering of grain and say together:

Ancestors, accept our offering!      Bydded Felly!

 

Deities

Dduwiau, gods and goddesses of the House of Don and gods and goddesses of the House of Llyr as well as the patron gods of all those gathered in this place.  Eldest and the wisest of spirits, greatest of guides, best of friends, and most nurturing of parents. On this night we give you our worship and ask that you join us at our hearthside.  Shining Ones, we give you this offering of whiskey and say together:

Shining Ones, accept our offering!  Bydded Felly!

 

Personal Offerings

PD: If anyone is called to make offering to their gods and allies, please do so now.

 

Reconnecting and Aligning

 

Deity of Occasion Invocation

PD:  We now call upon the Shining Bard of Wales to receive our offerings and praise.

 

Silencer of fools, who raises up the winds

Unlocking the chains of the fettered spirit.

Master of songspell, who seats the worthy,

And brings to humankind messages from the other worlds.

Divine Poet, Radiant Browed,

Who is placed in all time, in all places, in all things.

Of the harp string and bubble,

Of the sword and shield,

Of the spark and the wave.

 

Taliesin, Prophet and Magician, Merlin returned, we wish to do you honor here. 

If you will, Join us and Accept our Offering!   (Offering of special oil to the fire)

 

All:  Join us and accept our offering!   Bydded Felly!

 

 

Main Offering

A poem, song, and/or story are to be offered as well as a chant for all to participate in.  Food gathered from what was brought to the potluck will be offered at a special place of honor of Taliesin.  Part of the blessing cup will also be offered to him at the closing before the Earth Mother who will get the final portion.

Chant for Taliesin   -    By Z.L. Bennett

 

We strengthen and remember you,

Taliesin, Taliesin!

Most Noble Bard, we honor you,

Taliesin, Taliesin!

We fan the fire and sing the songs,

Taliesin, Taliesin!

That your tales be told for ages long,

Taliesin, Taliesin!

That your tales be told for ages long,

Taliesin, Taliesin!

That your tales be told for ages long.  (Repeat – build with drumming)

 

 (Offering of food being made during the chant, those gathering the food will dance it in and offering to the place of honor made for Taliesin).

 

Special Praise Offerings from the Folk

PD: Let those of you who have brought forth offerings to the great Bard do so now.

 

Final Praise Offerings to the Kin of Taliesin

PD: 

Poet Supreme, who has mastered the power in the fiery drops of Awen to enlighten and advise humanity, We have offered from our hearts to you. It is right here then, in your presence, Taliesin, to honor those of your kin who have allowed the spark of transformation to work through them so they could bring to the world stories, poems, and songs, to make the divine more palpable to humankind. 

 

     Each of us here holds in our hearts, heroic bards who have illuminated our own divine possibilities and have inspired us to live them.  So too, through them, we receive the drops of Awen to gestate within us until we birth our divine fire into the world.

 

     As we pass this candle about the room, please state the name of 1-2 bards that you would like to acknowledge for their part in your life or in the life of others.  (poets, writers, playwrights, composers, musical artists).  Candle passed.

 

The Omen

PD: We have honored the powers and have given offering and praise. As we strengthen them, so they too, in turn, strengthen us.  As we now have drawn them close here, let us open to the messages and blessings they wish to bestow upon us. (Lisa)

 

Blessing Cup

PD: With the omen received, we now prepare for the indwelling of their blessings within us. 

We call for the blessing of the Ancient Ones. Together we bid…Shining Ones, give us the waters!

We join our hearts, that each of us be blessed. Together we call… Shining Ones, give us the waters!

And as we are blessed, let all the worlds be blessed. Together we pray …Shining Ones, gives the waters of life!

(PD) Do you all accept these blessings of …..?  Behold the waters of life!    

Pour the Waters  - sung

 

Thanking

PD: “ We have given to the powers and the powers have given to us. Let us now thank them before they depart.”

Taliesin, Great Bard, and all the kin of your divine flame, for bringing the divine to us and joining us here, Diolch i chi, we thank you!  We offer a portion of the remaining Blessings here in this cup to sustain you.

Beloved Allies, who the folk gathered here this night, for your presence and care, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Shining Ones. Divine Ones who bless our lives, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Ancestors. Honored Ancient Ones who came before us, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Nature Spirits. Wild ones of the green world and those beyond the mists, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Cerridwen. Keeper of the cauldron of inspiration, for inspiring us, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Lleu Llaw Gyffes. Lleu of the deft arm, for watching over us, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Earth Mother. We return to you all blessings we could not contain. May you drink deep and grow stronger by doing so. For always holding firm beneath us, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

Gwyn ap Nudd, Lord of the world beyond. Lord of the Tylwyth Teg. For aiding us in keeping the ways open during our rite, we say to you, Diolch i chi, we thank you!

    And now join your magic with ours once more.

Let us together make this well but water.

Let us together make this fire but flame.

Let us together make this sacred tree no more than the stave of wood before us.

Let the Gates be closed!

 

Grounding /Recentering

 

 

Closing and Final Blessing

 

Let us end as we began. (Chime 3x3)

 

Brothers and Sisters, the rite has ended. Let us go forth inspired and warmed in the blessings of the gods and our community gathered here.  Bydded felly!
