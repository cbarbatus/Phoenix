PRE-RITUAL

 

●     Describe the challenge of recreating gaulish ritual due to lack of concrete history, explaining that while much of the ritual is inspired by personal gnosis as well as limited historical fact, that we believe devotion to the Kindred is valuable in any dress, be it whole or patchwork.

●     Teach “Come We Now As A People” procession song

●     Teach pronounciation of “Su esti” meaning “So be it.” or, more literally “It is good.”

●     Make clear the events of the ritual and potential sensitivities.

○     Smoke & Open Flame

○     Loud noise (Due to revelry. If neighbors are particularly loud it may be very noisy)

○     Mineral/Salt water hand bath

○     Any questions or concerns please speak with a member of the Grove before we begin

○     Standing for a long while (a chair can be provided)

○     We do not cast circles. If you need to leave at any time you may but we ask that you please be respectful and discreet about it.

○     Please be respectful to all involved and avoid interruptions during the ritual.

○     Comfort and consent is very important to us and essential to effective ritual work.

Receiving the Blessings

 

Lead 1:

Children of the earth, all of the offerings we have made have led to this moment. This is magic time! We have made offerings to the Kindreds and now will ask for their blessings in return. Please, visualize now your needs and desires, those blessings you wish to call for from the Kindreds. See it clearly in your mind’s eye. When we ask for the Waters of Life, when we speak “Give us the Waters”, see those blessings pour and descend into the upraised cup, like a thick, dancing mist, filling the cup. And when we drink of the Waters you will then receive your blessing. So please, close your eyes if you are able and take a moment to visualize your needs and the needs of our Grove, that we may all receive the blessings we desire.

Working

 

A priest/priestess of Sulis takes the healing bath from the purification procession and takes it in their embrace.

 

Lead 1:

Sulis [suh-lis] was honored by our ancestors for not only shining her bright light down upon them, filling their days with life. And She was honored not only for warming their weary muscles and aching bones with divine, healing bath water. She, too was called upon to grant the wishes of her followers. Wishes were left specifically for Sulis herself to grant, and we can see through these ancient acts the small nascent glimmer of more modern traditions such as wishing wells and fountains.

 

Tonight each of you was given a bright, shining coin. Our priest(ess) of Sulis [suh-lis] will raise the very bath we all cleansed ourselves in this evening and carry it around to all of you so that you may make a wish to Sulis [suh-lis] directly. When you have whispered your desire into the coin, please cast it into the bath as our priest(ess) comes to you.

 

Priest carries the bath around, accepting wishes, as music plays. Once this is done the priest holds the bath up high with the assistance of another druid holding the Antlers of Sulis up behind them to either side. The bath and the antlers are placed carefully near the fire as the music ends.
