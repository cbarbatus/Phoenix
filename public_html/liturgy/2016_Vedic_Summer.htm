HONORING THE EARTH MOTHER
 
Aeries: She is full-breasted; Her motherhood is a ceaseless creation. Her disheveled hair forms a
Kali Ma.  Motherhood in a ceaseless creation.  Consort of Siva [pronounced Shee-va]].  Your disheveled hair forms a curtain of illusion, the fabric of space – time which organizes matter out of the chaotic sea of quantum-foam.

Your garland of fifty human heads, symbolizes the repository of knowledge and wisdom.

Your girdle of severed human hands – the instruments of work and the action of karma – help us overcome the binding effects of this karma, severed by devotion to you.

Your red, lolling tongue symbolizes how you consume all things and taste the joy of what society regards as forbidden

Your four arms represent the complete circle of creation and destruction, which is contained within you, o’ great creative and destructive rhythms of the cosmos.

Your right hands, make the sign of mudras, symbolizing “fear not” and conferring boons.  Your left hands hold a bloodied sword and a severed head represent your destructive aspect, symbolizing the destruction of ignorance and the dawning of knowledge. Knowledge that cuts the knots of ignorance and destroys false consciousness.

Our ego sees Mother Kali and trembles with fear because our ego sees in your its own eventual demise.  Opens the gates of freedom with this sword, Kali Ma, having cut the eight bonds that bind human beings.

KEY OFFERINGS

The story
 
Lisa:  Today, on this Midsummer Day, we honor the glorious sun in his incarnation as the Lord Vishnu and his consort, the Goddess Lakshmi.  Our bards and dancers will join us as we learn the tail of the lovers.

The Gods, defeated by the Asuras, the demons,
Due to Indra’s hubrice and Sage Durvasa’s curse,
came to Lord Vishnu, seeking his assistance. 
Vishnu heard their plea and agreed to help the
Gods regain their strength and power. 
He advised the Gods, the Suras, to join with
their enemies and make the drink of immortality, Amrita.
He told them to gather herbs
of all kinds on the earth and
throw them with milk into the sea.
He told them to bring the Mandara Mountain to the sea
To be used as a churning stick and the
Serpent God, Vasuki, would be the rope that churned the sea.
He promised that though the Asuras would take part
In the work, they would not take part in the reward.
Emile: 
The Gods set to work carrying out the work
Prescribed by Vishnu.  Vishnu appeared three times
To help in the work.  Once to help move the mountain,
Once to help churn the sea, and once to distract the
Asuras in the guise of a beautiful woman, while the Suras
consumed the draught of immortality.
During the churning of the sea, many things emerged -
The sacred cow, Surabhi; the Goddess of wine, Varuni;
fair Parijata, the tree of paradise. 
the Moon, Mahadeva; a terrible poison claimed by
the serpent Gods themselves, The Th’ Apsarasas;
Aranyani, leading a troop of celestial nymphs with a garland of flowers;
Then, seated on a lotus, Beauty's bright goddess,
peerless Lakshmi, Sri [Shree], arose
Out of the waves; and with her, robed in white,
Came forth Dhanvantari, the gods’ physician.
High in his hand he bore the cup of nectar—
Life-giving draught, Amrita—longed for by gods and demons

Lisa: 
          Though the success of the Gods and Demons
          Was at hand, none could look away from the beauty of
          Lakshmi.  The troop of nymphs and Aranyani, danced in ecstasy, presenting Lakshmi with the immortal garland, as, one by one, the Suras and Asuras, the Gods and Demons, approached her to offer their hand in marriage,

as they knew Lakshmi engaged in the swayamwara,
the custom at the time of a woman of marriageable age,
choosing her own husband.  One-by-one she turned them away. 
Then, she saw the dark Lord Vishnu, turned away from the spectacle.

She pursued him, intrigued by his immunity to her beauty. 
She approached him again and again, yet he refused he,
Until he turned and she saw the twinkling of mischief in his eyes.
He accepted her garland of immortal flowers and they were married
beside the churning sea.  Thus, the Goddess of Wealth and Prosperity
joined with the Preserving God.  Let them increase and
preserve our wealth and prosperity through the growing
time of summer and the harvest time to come.
               
 

 

Invocations of the Deities of the Occasion
 
VISHNU
Emile:  
In the beginning of creation, the great Vishnu, desirous of creating the whole world, became threefold; Creator, Preserver, Destroyer. In order to create this world, the Supreme Spirit produced from the right side of his body himself as Brahmā; then, in order to preserve the world, he produced from his left side Vishnu; and in order to destroy the world, he produced from the middle of his body the eternal Siva. Some worship Brahmā, others Vishnu, others Siva; but Vishnu, one yet threefold, creates, preserves, and destroys: therefore let the pious make no difference between the three.
We ask you to join us today in your form of Lord Vishnu.

Lakshmi
Lisa:
As the lord of the worlds, the god of gods, Janārddana descends amongst mankind in various shapes; so does his coadjutor Sri [pronounced Shree]. Thus, when Hari was born a dwarf, Lakshmi appeared from the lotus as Padmā; when he was born as Rāma of the race of Bhrigu, she was Dharāni; when he was Rāghava, she was Sita; and when he was Krishna, she was Rukmini. If he takes a celestial form, she appears as divine; if a mortal, she becomes a mortal too, transforming her own person agreeably to partner his current incarnation.
We ask you to join us Lakshmi, Sri [pronounced Shree], beloved consort of Vishnu.  Mother who rose from the sea along with the nectar of immortality.  She who chose Vishnu as her own.
