INITIATING THE RITE
 
The folk assemble outside the nemeton to observe the preparations censed (Aeries) as entering archway.
 Folk take a color scarf of the clan they most associate with.
A chant is sung as they process toward the site.

Come we gather at the water, come we gather at the tree, at the fire we join as a people, to the grove with you and me.

Procession and Purification
Heather: Druids, release the cares of your mundane life.  Take a deep breath in and allow your mind to be still.  Thoughts outside of this time are released.  You are here spiraling ever forward in the present.
 
Mike: Feel the pull of our sacred space, calling you, whispering on the wind.  In this space we create our sacred realities.  We can become all the Kindreds believe us to be.  Purification allows us to transform into our sacred selves.  Please come forward, be purified, and attune yourself to mythic time.  Let go and cross the divide.

Heather:  Story of Lugh:

Tommi steps forward as Balor
It all begins when Balor, King of the Formorians heard a prophecy from a druid who revealed: Heather as Druid: "You will come to be slain by your own grandson."
Balor thought, as many people do, that he could avoid his fate by banishing his only daughter  Ethlin stepps forward Ethlinn, away from the courtship of any young man. Though she was kept locked away, fate is not so easily defeated. It came to be, that Kian, Kian finds her of the Danu people still found his way to her. They embrace They lay together and soon, she was with child. Bundled scarf for baby

When Balor heard of this, he was enraged. He called for the execution of her babies of which there were three, but one managed to escape the grip of death. Bundle is passed to story teller This child was Lugh. Lost to the waves, Mananan takes Bundle Mananan Mac Lir found him and took him under his tutalige on the Country of Mananaun. There he grew up and grew rich in skills. Not only thoes of smithy, bard, and scholar, but in the magical arts as well.
Ken steps forward as Lugh and gets the sash Lugh has come of age and, growing homesick, wished to return to his land. It is during this time that the great ones of the Danu people are being oppressed, turned into slaves in their own land. Lugh decides it is time for him to come to the aide of his father's people.
He made his way to the great door of the palace where a discerning doorkeeper sat. Heather as Doorkeeper:"No one may enter unless we need what you can offer. What can you do?"
Lugh: "I am a carpenter."
Doorkeeper: "We already have a great carpenter."
Lugh: "I am a smith."
Doorkeeper: "We have inside a master smith."
Lugh: "Then a warrior."
Doorkeeper: "We are protected by Ogma, the great warrior."
Lugh tried all his many skills, Physician, harper, poet, spencer, the list went on but there was already a person of great accomplishment in whichever art he offered to the court. Finally, he thought of something that was missing.
Lugh: "Ask the king if he has any in his service who can say they are masters of all of these at once."
That they did not have and he was allowed in. Lugh brought with him several magical items that he had been gifted with upon his departure from Mananaun. He had Mananan's boat which could sail through land as if on water, and his horse, which could walk above the waves as if on land, the terrible sword Fragarach, that could cut through any chainmail or armor, and of course the spear of Lugh which never misses its mark. Lugh is given spear
Lugh rides out to meet Balor on the battlefield, the destined confrontation. Ken and Tommi ride out and meet and begin to pantomine a fight. They are evenly matched, and the fight is friece, but Lugh lifts his long spear and strikes Balor right through the eye. Spear is shoved through Eye bread and tossed in fire Balor is defeated, peace can reign and the great ones are restored.
