Imbolc 2006

Written by Nicole Harlow

(Inspired by many…)

 

Call to the Rite

Thrice three (9) chimes announce the rite.

 

Outdwellers

Female Warrior: Forces of chaos, you who dwell in the places beyond our firelight, beyond our gates, and the dark places between, you who are of the untamed wild, the primal and unbounded, Outdwellers stay beyond our gates!  We offer this mead to you and ask that you not disturb us during this rite.

 

Bíodh sé Amhlaidh!

 

Brideog Procession

First flamekeeper enters the nemeton, and stands by the unlit fire.  The women enter the nemeton first, holding the Brideog doll.

 

Chant:

“We approach the sacred grove

With hearts and minds and flesh and bone

Join us now in ways of old

We have come home”

 

When all the women have entered, and the Brideog doll has been placed on her bed, PD says:

 

Brighid has come, Brighid is welcome!

 

The men respond: We would enter to honour Brighid!

 

All women: Welcome!  Welcome!  Welcome, O Brighid!

 

Chant:

“We approach the sacred grove

With hearts and minds and flesh and bone

Join us now in ways of old

We have come home”

 

When all the men have passed through, the other two flamekeepers join the first flamekeeper and light the flame, PD and Druids give and introduction honouring Brighid.

 

Introduction

PD: We gather here today to honor Brighid, Goddess of Fire, Goddess of Healing and Goddess of Inspiration.  This is the Feast of Imbolc, our time for renewal with the Earth Mother.

 

Druid 1: This is the season of the first milk for the lambs and when the Winter starts its turn towards Spring.

 

Druid 2: We are all part of the cycle, with the end meeting with the beginning, and life is once again renewed.

 

Druid 3: As those who came before us, as we do now, we come to the Grove to honour and worship as they have.

 

Druid 4: The seeds that have been planted under our feet, begin to spring forth, and push their way through the earth.

 

PD: So let us join here today together as one to make our offerings to Brighid, our Ancestors, the Sidhe, and the Gods!

 

Bíodh sé Amhlaidh!

 

Call for Inspiration

PD: Bright Brighid, we call for your inspiration for this rite!

 

Bards:

O Brigid, Dear Lady,

Grant us your presence here this day.

For we are, your people,

Keeping your flame in our special way.

 

From the anvil and healing waters,

From the poet’s sacred song,

Forge our hearts, worthy of your protection

Where champions are safe and steel is strong.

 

O Brigid, Warm Mother,

Inspire all hearts for peace today.

For we are, your people

Sharing your flame in our special way.

 

From the nourishing milk of all mothers,

From your golden sparkling light,

Warm our hearts, worthy of revelation

Where dreams are born to soar the heights.

 

O Brigid, Dear Lady,

Grant us your presence here this day.

For we are your people,

Keeping your flame in our special way.

Keeping your flame in our special way.

Keeping your flame in our special way.

 

Call for Peace/Earth Mother

Druid 1: Here in the east, here there shall be peace and prosperity!

 

Druid 2: Here in the south, here there shall be peace and harmony!

 

Druid 3: Here in the west, here there shall be peace and wisdom!

 

Druid 4: Here in the north, here there shall be peace and strength!

 

Druid 5: Earth mother, here in the center we honor your body, from you we are formed and to you we shall return!  It is here we are joined as your children, wrapped in your bounty and in your beauty.  It is here we honour you!

(Druid offers grain to the earth)

 

Bíodh sé Amhlaidh!

 

Three Hallows

PD: We will now consecrate the hallows by which we make offering!

 

Well

Druid: Sacred Well

Deep well of wisdom

Waters of life beneath us

Sacred source, rivers, lakes and oceans

It is here we honor you, uisci naomh (ish-kuh neev)

(an offering of silver is made to the well)

Sacred Well, flow within us!

People Respond: Sacred Well, flow within us!

 

Fire

Druid: Sacred Fire

Light of transformation

Creator of order out of chaos

Flames of inspiration and illumination

It is here we honor you, tine naomh (chinneh neev)

(an offering of oil is made to the fire)

Sacred Fire, burn within us!

People Respond: Sacred Fire, burn within us!

 

Tree

Druid: Sacred Tree

Connector of the worlds

You whose roots reach deep below

And whose branches touch the skies above

It is here we honor you, crann naomh (krahn neev)

(an offering of water from the well, and incense is made to the tree)

Sacred Tree, grow within us!

People Respond: Sacred Tree, grow within us!

 

Sanctifying the Participants

PD: Let us make ourselves ready for the working of this rite.

 

(Druids cense and asperge)

 

All: Fire in the Heart, fire of the mind

Fire on the wind, fire out of time

 

Fire of the night, fire of the soul

Fire pure and bright, fire make us whole.

(Repeats until finished)

 

Opening the Gates

PD: We now call on Mannanan Mac Lir, our gatekeeper, to open the gates and open the way to the Otherworlds!

 

Druid: Mannanan Mac Lir

Gatekeeper, open the gates!

Sidhe King, open the gates!

Rider of the waves, open the gates!

Walker of the mists, open the gates!

Mannanan, we call to you, open the gates!

 

All: Mannanan, Mannanan, Mannanan… (repeat until final call)

 

Druid: Osclaítear na geataí! (OS-kluh-TEER na GAH-thay)

Let the Gates be open!

 

Three Kindred

PD: Let us now give honor to the Three Kindred, so they may honour us in return.

 

Nature Spirits

A sprideanna na talaimh (uh SPREE-jun-na na TALL-eev), spirits of the land!

You who dwell deep within the land, and all around
You who spread your wings above, you of feather
You who walk the ground, the hunters and the hunted
You who swim the seas, scaled and furred alike
You creatures, who live in our world and the worlds beyond

We offer you these gifts, so you may join us in this rite!

(Druid makes an offering)

Nature spirits, accept our sacrifice!

 

All: Nature spirits, accept our sacrifice!

 

Ancestors

A shinseara (uh HEEN-sher-un), mighty ancestors

You who have walked before us and guided us

You of kin and heart and blood

You, old ones of long ago

You who are our kindred and ancient blood

We offer you these gifts, so you may join us in this rite!

(Druid makes an offering)

Ancestors, accept our sacrifice!

 

All: Ancestors, accept our sacrifice!

 

Deities

A Dheithe (uh YAY-huh), Shining Ones

You, eldest and brightest, wisest and mightiest

You who are known to us and unknown

You, great and boundless ones

You bless our lives, you whom we worship here

 

We give you honor and offer you these gifts, so you may join us in this rite!

(Druid makes an offering)

Gods and Goddesses, accept our sacrifice!

 

All: Gods and Goddesses, accept our sacrifice!

 

Tale

PD: Angus and Bride (To be read from “book”)

 

Praise Offerings

PD: If the spirit should move you, let anyone who has praise come forth to make your praise offerings to Brighid, your kindred, or your patrons.

 

Main Offering

PD: At this time, let us join in our main offering to Brighid

 

The offering of Brighid’s Cross is made.  The Descent of Brighid is read while presenting the Brighid’s Cross.

 

PD: The Descent of Brighid
Brigid of the mantles, Brigid of the hearth fire,
Brigid of the twining hair, Brigid of the auguries
Brigid of the fair face, Brigid of the calmness
Brigid of the strong hands, Brigid of the kine
Brigid, friend of women, Brigid fire of magic
Brigid the foster mother, Brigid woman of wisdom.
Brigid the daughter of Danu, Brigid the triple flame.
Each day and each night I call the descent of Brigid.
That the power of healing be within us,
That the power of poetry be within us,
That the power of shaping be within us,
In earth, sea and sky and among all kindreds.
Kindle your flame in our heads, hearts and loins,
Make us your cup, your harp, your forge,
That we may heal, inspire and transform,
All in your honor, Brigid font of blessing.
Brigid above us, Brigid below us, Brigid at every airt about us.
Brigid in our truest heart.

 

Omen

PD: Seer, what blessings are offered to us in return?

 

Seer draws 3 ogham are drawn to see how offerings were received.

 

Candle Lighting

This is the time for those with candles to light them from the sacred fire, during which time, white ribbons are handed out to hang outside at night to receive Brighid’s blessings.

 

Blessing Cup

Druid: Blessed Brighid, Ancient and Shining Ones! We have offered to you, so now we ask for your gifts in return!

(Druid pours mead into the cup)

We seek your blessings, of wisdom, of magic, and of rebirth!

We seek your blessings, of prosperity, of plenty, and of love!

Kindreds, we ask that you give us the waters of life!

 

All: Give us the waters of life!

 

(The cup is passed around)

 

PD: As you share in this blessing, do not hesitate to salute or honor them, the gates are still open and offerings may still be made.  For those who choose not to drink, please feel free to honor in your own way.

 

Thanks

PD: We now give thanks to those who we have honored here and those who have aided us in our rite.

 

Brighid, Goddess of our inspirations!  We thank you!

All: We thank you!

 

Great Earth Mother, all that surrounds us!  We thank you!

All: We thank you!

 

Mannanan Mac Lir, our gatekeeper, opener of doors!  We thank you!

All: We thank you!

 

A sprideanna na talaimh (uh SPREE-jun na TALL-eev), spirits of the land!  We thank you!

All: We thank you!

 

A shinseara (uh HEEN-sher-un), mighty ancestors! We thank you!

All: We thank you!

 

A Dheithe (uh YAY-huh), Shining Ones!  We thank you!

All: We thank you!

 

PD: For all the blessings unused, we return to the Earth.  (PD pours remains of cup)

 

Closing the Gates

 

PD: We call upon Mannanan Mac Lir once again to close the gates.

 

Druid: Mannanan Mac Lir, Gatekeeper!

We thank you for guarding us and guiding us!

The well is now but water!

The fire is now but a flame!

And let the tree be but the very heart of us all that we carry forth from this place!

 

Mannanan Mac Lir!

Let the Gates now be closed!

 

PD: As it started, so shall it end.

 

Thrice three (9) chimes end the rite.

 

PD: Brothers and sisters, the rite has ended.
