Statement of Purpose

 

[Mike]: Tonight we gather between Earth and Sky, to honor and worship the Kindreds as one people. We sing their praises as make our sacrifice, as we seek their blessing in return.

 

[Jessica]: Croeso y Pob Bendith [croy-so uh pob ben-dith]. We gather this night to celebrate Midwinter. As those who have gone before us have done, let us celebrate the turning of the year in the company of community, gathered around a good fire in the cold of winter to weave tale and song, stirring the embers of the fires of the head to inspire the work of our hands in the promise of the New Year. We have come together to honor the powers that sustain us, especially the bards, those who harness inspiration to create beauty, myth and prophecy, in order to make the divine more palpable in this world. Statement of Purpose

 

[Jessica]:  

Tonight we will call to and toast the great bard, Taliesin, for his triumph as the greatest of all bards. Taliesin’s name means “radiant brow”, an allusion to his skillful mind and to the gift referred to in the Celtic world as the “fire in the head”, that is possessing a quick wit and a deft tongue to speak and craft works of poetry and song. In ancient British tradition, Taliesin was said to have been a bard in King Arthur’s court. He was also said to have been conceived magically of the goddess Cerridwen through a series of shapeshifting exchanges that took place over a year and a day. Tonight, we will delve into these bardic mysteries, and express ourselves in offering to the kindred as we make our toast!
