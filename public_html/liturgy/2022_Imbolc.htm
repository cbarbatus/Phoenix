STATEMENT OF PURPOSE

 

ELIZABETH: We gather here to celebrate Imbolc, the time before the birth of lambs when the ewes come into their milk. For some, Imbolc remains a day frozen in the grip of winter, when half the stores are gone, and the land is still bleak and quiet. But here, in this land, we recognize the stirring of life beneath our feet, as winter begins to depart, as the rivers flood, as the days begin to lengthen, as the first buds appear on the trees, and tulips push through the cold soil to greet the sun.

 

AIDEN: We are here to offer worship to the goddess Brigid: she who protects, heals, and inspires. At this time, renewal begins for mother earth; seeds take root and come to flower. As the light strengthens and awakens the land, let it be so for us as well; let us clear away the old, dead leaves of winter and prepare our soil to be planted anew, fresh and clear with intention and ripe with purpose.

 

ELIZABETH: As our ancestors did, so do we now: we are here to honor the Old Bargain between mortals and the Kindreds. So let us join together as one folk to make our offerings in joy and reverence. Bíodh sé amhlaidh!

KEY OFFERINGS

 

BARDS: [We Sing a Song To Brigid - Written by Clare Maloney and Patrica Hegarty]

 

ARIEL: Children of the earth, it is time to meet the Goddess Brigid.

 

Stand, sit, or lie down in a comfortable position. Take a deep breath, allowing that breath to cleanse you of all the day's troubles. Squeeze every muscle in your body. Hold it. Now release. Take a deep breath. Notice the swelling of your chest, the fullness in your lungs, and breathe out, letting your body simply relax into the release. Now take a third breath, and see yourself in a beautiful field, lush with grass and wildflowers. Cows low in the distance. A canvas of stars paints the sky above you. The moon is radiant and whole, and you allow it to guide your steps.

 

In time, your feet bring you to a grassy knoll, a brightly-lit house at its modest crest. The door, though closed, somehow feels almost to be beckoning you, and as you set foot near the threshold, that door opens to you.

 

The home within is a unique combination of comfort and beauty. A fire crackles in the hearth. There are smithing tools, musical instruments, and an impressive apothecary stocked full with all one could ever need to heal and do great works of magic. You feel at peace here. You feel at home.

 

"There you are," comes a voice from the hall, and as your gaze follows the sound, you see Her. Red hair and bright eyes, delicate yet calloused hands resting gently on the door's frame. There's an understated yet unmistakable radiance to Her very being.

 

Brigid. Bride. Brigantia. Even St. Brigit, to some. A Goddess of many names, known and worshipped by many different people.

 

"I've been expecting you," she says. "Come. Sit. You've had quite the journey, haven't you?"

 

There is such warmth to her smile. A knowing. A certain ease. You are welcome here, and in this moment, this is precisely where you belong.

 

The two of you settle down by the hearth, and as find your place, you feel a fire in your belly.

 

"Tell me, Child," she says. "What weighs on you?"

 

Pause

 

She listens to you, to everything you have to tell Her, and as you grow quiet again, the fire in your belly grows, expanding to your torso, down your arms and down your legs. It burns away all that you no longer need--the worries and cares of the world, the hurts and the limiting beliefs that no longer serve, transforming you. It's the healing waters that douse the flames of transformation, starting from the crown of your head, trickling down across your shoulders. It pools in the hollow of your throat, quenching that flame in your belly. You are healed where you most need it. Old wounds at last find relief. They are washed away, soaking deep, deep into the earth.

 

"And tell me," she says again, "What tool would you have to help you along your journey? To help you achieve all that you intend in the coming season?"

 

Pause

 

As you have asked, so you receive. She presses the tool to your hands. Your fingers close tight around it, and she draws you into her arms, strong yet gentle all at once. And as you bask in her warmth, she speaks to you--a message, just for you, to send you on your way.

 

Pause

 

You come back to yourself again. The house is gone. The cows are gone. The field is gone. There is only the moon, the lingering weight of Her embrace, and the comforting scent of fire and cloves. And yet, you still have Her words. You still have the tool she gave you. You are still healed and transformed, and your success is imminent.

 

Breathe in. Breathe out. Bring yourself back to our sacred space, back with all Brigid has given you, and open your eyes.
