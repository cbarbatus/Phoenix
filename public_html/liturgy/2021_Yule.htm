We have come together to honor the powers that sustain us, especially the bards, those who harness inspiration to create beauty, myth and prophecy, in order to make the divine more palpable in this world. KEY OFFERINGS

 

 

Statement of Purpose

 

Emily:  

Tonight we will call to and toast the great bard, Taliesin /tah-LYEH-sinn/, for his triumph as the greatest of all bards. Taliesin’s name means “radiant brow”, an allusion to his skillful mind and to the gift referred to in the Celtic world as the “fire in the head”, that is possessing a quick wit and a deft tongue to speak and craft works of poetry and song. In ancient British tradition, Taliesin was said to have been a bard in King Arthur’s court. He was also said to have been conceived magically of the goddess Cerridwen /keh-RID-wen/ through a series of shapeshifting exchanges that took place over a year and a day. Tonight, we will delve into these bardic mysteries, and express ourselves in offering to the kindred as we make our toast!
