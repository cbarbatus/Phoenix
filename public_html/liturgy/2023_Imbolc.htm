INITIATING THE RITE

LEAD: Druids, let us prepare to enter the Nemeton. Children of the earth! Release the cares of your mundane life. Breathe deeply and let your mind be still. Thoughts outside this time are released. You are here rooted deeply in the present.

CO-LEAD: Feel the pull of our sacred space, calling out to you, whispering on the wind. Here, we can become all the Kindreds call us to be. Purification enables our transformation into our sacred selves. Join us, be purified, and attune yourself to mythic time. (Rings large chime 3x3, to signal the start of ritual, & also the pitch for entrance chant)
(2 Druids hold oak branches in an arch in the entryway)
(2 more druids get ready to cense and asperge as the folk process back toward the Nemeton)


PROCESSION AND PURIFICATION
(Procession begins with the chimes and the bards going first initiating the chant and 1 or 2 keeping the tempo up with a string of small Indian bells, very softly jingling them.
Followed by 1 or 2 more druids holding aloft Brigid's crosses Placing them on the altar when applicable and the rest of the procession)

Branch Druids (in Unison as people pass through) Be purified as you pass through the oaken arch into mythic time.

STATEMENT OF PURPOSE:

LEAD & CO-LEAD (begin to circle the hallows in a symbiotic dance and alternate the following)

LEAD: We approach the eve of Imbolc, the spoke on the Wheel of the Year that celebrates the revival of life for the Earth Mother and her inhabitants.

CO-LEAD: The sun reaches ever higher in the sky, warming the Earth, awakening her from a long winter hibernation.

LEAD: Seeds planted begin to take root. Plant yourself firmly like the Cosmic Tree and feel new life replenish your soul.

CO-LEAD: Look to the brightening sky, that your aspirations may reach up like seedlings breaking through the snow.

LEAD: May we be refreshed and renewed as we rejoice in ritual and song in honor of the Goddess Brigid /BRIDGE-id/, the goddess of fire, inspiration, and healing.

CO-LEAD: As our ancestors did, so do we now: we are here to honor the Old Bargain between mortals and the Kindreds.

TOGETHER: So, let us join together as one folk to make our offerings in joy and reverence. Bíodh sé amhlaidh! /BEE-uh SHAY OWL-ee/

THE TALE OF BRIGID’S MANTLE

LEAD: Now is the time to welcome in the Goddess Bridgid. Bard, would you begin by spinning the tale of Brigid's mantle?
Mike: Mantle is another word for cape, or cloak...In one of the well-known tales about Brigid, she felt called to establish a retreat, a center of welcome, hospitality and peace where all people, especially the poor, could come and find nourishment for life's journey. She went to the king of Leinster /LEN-ster/ to ask him for some land on which to establish her center. She told him that the place where they stood was the perfect spot: it was beside an oak grove where she and her companions could collect firewood and berries, there was a lake nearby that would provide water, and the land was fertile, good for raising sheep and cows.
The king, not renowned for generosity, was hesitant and asked her how much land she'd need. Oh, just as much as I can cover with my mantle, replied Brigid. This meager request pleased the king, so he readily agreed. Thereupon Brigid took off her mantle and laid it on the ground. And miraculously, the mantle began to GROW! It grew, and it grew, and much to the king's astonishment, it continued to grow, until it covered several acres of rich and beautiful land. The king was dismayed, but honor prevented him from going back on his word. And so was established the town of Cill Dara /Kill DAH-ra/, Irish for the church of the oak, present-day Kildare, /Kill DARE/ where to this day the Brigidine /BRIDGE-ih-DEEN/ sisters keep alight the perpetual flame dedicated to Brigid.

LEAD: Bards, please gather at the harp and enjoy this bardic offering of song as we now invite the Goddess of the occasion, in the person of the Brídeóg.  (The doll is veiled until the finish of the song.)

HALLOWING THE BLESSINGS

CO-LEAD: Let us visualize these blessings pouring through the sacred portals and flowing into the Blessing cup. (Pause for a moment, and softly ring Indian bells.)

LEAD: O Mighty Kindreds, hallow these waters. Grant us the blessings we seek. Let the wisdom, love and power of the Gods, the Dead and the Sidhe /shee/ flow in this cup of blessing.
