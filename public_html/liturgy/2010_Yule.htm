Statement of Purpose

 

PD:  We gather together tonight as a community to celebrate Midwinter.  We will do so by honoring the age old tradition of Wassailing.  Some of you may have heard of Wassailing as a caroling tradition, and a time to drink heartily and be merry.  Yes, Wassailing does have song and drink and merriment, but the custom is so much deeper than just revelry.  Wassailing, like many other holiday traditions we celebrate today, originate from pre-Christian, pagan customs and rituals.  In the lands now known as Britain, on Twelfth Night, people gathered in the apple orchards to toast the good health of the apple trees.  The toasting protected the dormant roots, and awakened the spirit of the apple tree for a bountiful crop.  The word Wassail is a greeting meaning “be in good health.”  So, the tradition to Wassail the apple trees, has evolved to a tradition of singing carols and toasting each other for good health in the upcoming year.

We each will have an opportunity to Wassail the apple tree.  We will also call to and offer to the Goddess, Arianrhod, for she is the bright lady of the season.  By doing our work together tonight, we will be part of the magic of her turning of the wheel.  Arianrhod's turning of the wheel is quite different from the turning of the wheel of the year, that some of you may be accustomed to.  Arianrhod spins the wheel of fate, her wheel is used to weave destinies.  Arianrhod translates to “Silver Wheel”.  In Welsh tradition, the Silver Wheel correlates to Arianrhod's castle, Caer Arianrhod.  Here, a set of stars rotate around the Pole Star.  Souls go to Caer Arianrhod to await their next incarnation. 

Tonight, we will offer to and share in the magic of Arianrhod's spinning wheel.

Main Offering to Arianrhod

 

PD:  We have called Arianrhod to join us in our working this night.  With her help, we will now Wassail the Apple Tree!  Arianrhod hosts souls as they await reincarnation.  We will help the dormant apple tree reawaken so her next year crops of apples will be bountiful.  By doing so, we will be honoring Arianrhod's work of her turning wheel. 

Let us being with the tradition of song.

 

[ Song – Gloucestershire Wassail – Sung in the key C major ]

 

PD:  We have stirred the roots of the apple tree with song.  Let us now speak words of offering to the apple tree.

 

As the Wassail Bowl is passed, please say what you offer to awaken the dormant Apple Tree.  Your offering can be a specific attribute, it can be good luck, or it can be a specific offering. Some examples are, “I offer strength”, “I offer protection”, “I offer that I will go out in nature once a day until Imbolc”  These are just examples.  Think about what  your gifts are, and incorporate those into your offering.

 

[Wassail Bowl passed]

 

PD:  Now that we have heard song  and spoken words of offering, let us follow in the last bit of tradition and raise the energy into the Wassail Bowl for the final offering.  Sing, tone, scream, drum, whatever you are called to do, let us make a racket to awaken the Apple Tree!!!

 

[Energy Raised]
