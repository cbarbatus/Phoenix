Guardian

Paul: The days grow shorter still, and night comes fast upon us now. In this time of darkness we are here to welcome in the last harvest, to enjoy the final fruits of our labor before the coming harshness of Winter. In elder days the Folk knew that spirits were about this night, and they made offerings that the sprites might be gentle. They knew that on this night the Dead are with us and must be given their due, lest they reward our arrogance with mischief of their own. So, on this night let us remember our Ancestors and all our Honored Dead, let us celebrate with the Nature Spirits around us, and let us honor the Shining Ones, so we may enjoy the Kindreds’ blessings through the darkness of Winter.

Susan:
Croeso y Pob Bendith /KROY-saw uh POBE ben-DITH/ . As we have reached the end of the cycle of the year and the final harvest, we gather tonight to celebrate the most sacred of holidays, Samhain. The days shorten, the cool winds begin to blow, and Winter is upon us.  We are at the time of the year where the veil is thin and we can commune with our beloved departed.  However, we can also rejoice in the new cycle upon us for it is our New Year. HAPPY NEW YEAR! Let us stand together as a community tonight and revel in the time to renew, to begin again.


Paul:
As those who have gone before us have done, let us celebrate the turning of the year in the company of community, gathered around a good fire in the first winds of winter to weave tale and song, stirring the embers of the fires of the head to inspire the work of our hands in the promise of the New Year. As our ancestors did and our children may do in times to come, let us honor the Mighty Ones in the old ways. Welcome to the turning of the year, the night and day between the years, the feast of Samhain. At Samhain, the sidhe /SHE/ mounds are opened and the dead commune with the living. Let us this night honor the Mighty Dead and the seeds of life that lie in the belly of death in the old ways.
