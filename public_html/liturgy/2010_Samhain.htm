Samhain Ritual
Raven's Cry Grove, ADF
Written by Anthony Thompson and adapted/written by Z.L. Bennett
For public rite November 2010

I. Establishing the Grove

Musical signal is given (Chimes 3x3)

Call on Protector  (Standing at the North)

D1: Golden Lugh, of the sure hand and long arm,

       Your people call upon you this night

       To lend your protection to this rite and on our journey home. 

       Bold Hero, of shadow and light, ward our steps here and help us to forge right alliance.
       We offer this in friendship and gratitude.   [ale offering]

       Samildanach, accept our sacrifice.   ALL: Repeat

Outdwellers (Standing at the South)

D1:  We offer this to the energies that would pit their will against ours,

       And though our ways are as foreign to you as yours are to us,

       We offer this in respect and ask that you trouble neither this rite, nor those gathered here.  [offering of grain]

 

       Outdwellers, accept our sacrifice.  ALL: Repeat!


PD1: Brothers and Sisters, you may now enter the Nemeton.

Processional: We Approach the Sacred Grove

We approach the sacred grove
With hearts and minds and flesh and bone
Join us now in ways of old
We have come home


Opening Statement

PD1: Támuid anseo leis na Déithe a adhradh. (Tah mweed ahn-shau ley-sh nah Yay-huh uh ah-rah)
     We are here to honor the gods. So, let us pray with a good fire. Let us offer with a full heart knowing that when we do, we nurture our relationship with them. In remembering, we strengthen and sustain them. In honor, we balance the worlds, bring peace, and keep the Old Ways alive and present.

     Samhain has come.  The days grow shorter and the nights come sooner. In this time of opening darkness, we come here to celebrate the end of the harvest – to enjoy the final fruits of our labor before the waiting, harsh winter arrives.  In elder days, the Folk knew that many spirits were about; and so, our Ancestors made offerings that the spirits might be gentle. They knew the Dead were close and that they must be given due honor, lest the dead reward our arrogance with mischief of their own. Our elders knew of their tie and duty to their beloved Ancestors, and so, they kept their oaths in honor to those of the past while providing a bridge to those of the future.

So, during this most sacred of times,

Let us honor the spirits of nature and the powers who hold the mysteries of this time. 

Let us remember our Ancestors and our Honored Dead.

Let us bless those who will pass and those who will come this year,

And let us examine the oaths that we uphold knowing that they are the foundation we build upon for the future. 

Through our work this night and the truth of inner reflection that this season requires of us,

May we come to enjoy the Kindreds' blessings through the darkness of winter and the dark times of our lives.

Earth Mother

PD2: We begin first, as we must, by honoring our beloved Earth Mother.

D1: Earth Mother, our lives are nursed from your bounty.

      You are the food that nourishes us, the wealth that enriches us, the very ground that supports us.
      O Great Mother of many names, all comes from you,

      And it is you who sustains us no matter your weariness or pain,
      Earth Mother, join us here, as we offer you a song from our grateful hearts.

Chant to Earth Mother: Blossom Lifter (1x)

1. Earth Mother, Blossom Lifter
Bloom with the rye,
Bloom with the wheat,
Bloom with barley,
And bloom with all grains.


2.Earth Mother, Blossom Lifter
Bless what we eat,
Bless what we drink,
Bless what we harrow,
And bless what we sow

3.Earth Mother, Blossom Lifter
With all these things
Let us rejoice.
You give to us,
And we give to you.

D1: (After song, druid makes an offering of rosewater to the center)
      Earth Mother, accept our offering!  (ALL Repeat)


Call For Inspiration

PD2: We now call upon the powers of inspiration to aid us in our rite.

Bright Brid, Daughter of the Daghdha, Flame of Inspiration,
Metal crafter, Healer, Maker of sweet song,
With you as our mother, we need fear no illness,
With you as our mother, we need fear no dull swords,
With you as our mother, we need fear no empty words.
Our tribe is triple-blessed, O shining light of the hearth,
and we your children ask you to alight in our hearts,
Alight in our heads, and alight in our voices,
That we may give praise to the gods with good skill.

[Offer oil to the fire and to the anvil near the fire and then hit 3x.]
Brigid, Accept our offering. !    ALL: Brigid, Accept our offering.

Imbas upon us!                      ALL: Imbas upon us!

Grove Attunement

     PD2:  With our Earth Mother and the powers of inspiration present, let us now attune ourselves to the elements around us and 

               within us.

 

PD1: -  Two Powers Meditation

PD1: closes with Unity Chant (adapted) - spoken at end of meditation:
We are one, in this grove,
upon the land, within the sea, beneath the sky.
We are joined, to send our call,
to the honored dead, to the spirit folk, to the blessed gods.
We praise the Kindreds three, in one voice, from one center, in this grove.
We are one and we set apart this space as sacred.


Establishing the Hallows

 

PD2:  As we stand here purified and prepared, let us now prepare the hallows where we will make offering.
[All three Druids sanctifying the hallows will come forward at the same time and stand near their hallow.]

Well
D1:  O sacred waters that flow and swirl beneath all being,
       Well of ancient wisdom, keeper of deep riches,
       Be blessed with our offering! O great source of all,
       Let us know the elder depths within ourselves.
       In the depths flow the waters of wisdom.

[Offers silver to Well and takes up some water in a vessel for aspersing the tree hold up and say…]

       Sacred waters flow within us.  All: Repeat.

Fire
D1:  O sacred fire that consumes and transforms,
       True and holy light of the Shining Ones,
       Be blessed with our offering! O sacrificed and sacrificer,
       Let your holy flame warm our spirits and our lives.

[Offer oil to the fire, then light a stick incense from the flame, hold up while still aflame and say….]

       Sacred fire, burn within us.  All: Repeat.   [Both the fire and well druids walk to the tree with their items]

Tree
D1:  O sacred pillar, boundary of all worlds,
       Stand at the center of the sky,
       Stand at the center of the sea,
       Stand at the center of the land on which we dwell.
       Let us find depth, as you are deep,
       Let us rise, as you reach high,
       And let us be strong, as you stand strong.

[Druids with water and incense asperse & cense the world tree]

       Sacred tree, grow within us.     All: Repeat.

Opening the Gates

PD2: Now that this grove is claimed and hallowed, let us now call upon our Gate Keeper to bring the worlds together

         and to open the gates of the sacred center.

Oh Manannan MacLir, powerful son of the sea, holder of the magic of the crane bag, we ask you to hear our call.
Oh Lord of the Otherworld, bearer of the silver apple branch, join with us this day.
We ask that you guide us safely in our workings as you have done many times before.
Handsome mist-shrouded rider of the maned waves,

Accept now this offering of silver, and let us together open the gates.
[a silver triskel is offered to the well]
Manannan, accept our offering!

People chant Manannan or other, raising energy.

Let the fire open as a gate,  (Grove sacrificer offers at each hallow as it is spoken)
let the well open as a gate,
and let the tree connect all the worlds
as Manannan walks with us in all ways.

Osclaítear na geataí anois! (os-kluh-TEER na GA-thay a-NISH!)
Let the gates be opened!  All:  Repeat

III. Offering to the Powers

Honoring and Inviting the Three Kindreds

PD1: Let us now call on the Kindred so we may honor them.

Nature Spirits

D1: A sprideanna na talaimh!
Spirits of the Good Gentry and the noble trees that keep and teach us,
Spirits of stream, stone, wind, sand, and soil,
Spirits of the fiery realms of sun, moon, and star,
Spirits of the earth, of fur and fin, scale, feather, and skin,
Nature Spirits, you who balance, heal, comfort, trick, teach, and inspire, we call to you.

O Noble Ones! We call to you and with sincerity, and we offer this!
[flowering herbs offered to the tree]

Join us here, and accept our sacrifice!           ALL: (Repeat)

Deities

D1: A Dheithe! –
Divine Ones, both known to us and unknown,
Those who hold the powers of this place,
Patrons of all those here,
You who speak to what is grand in all the worlds,
And do such deeds that win our hearts and praise.
You reveal the delicate tapestries of fate and will
And teach the most profound of lessons,
How could we go on dull and indifferent?
When our souls long to know you and do you honor?

Shining Ones! With open hearts, ever mindful of the mutual charge between us.
We offer this to you!   [oil offering to fire]

Join us here, and accept our sacrifice!         ALL: (Repeat)


Praise Offerings of the Folk
PD2: At this time, let those who would offer praise and thanks to the spirits of nature or their gods and allies, do so.  Offering to the Ancestors will be done at a later time.  (Drumming)

Ritual Purpose, and Honoring the Spirits of the Occasion

PD1: In this time, when the Otherworld is near and the ways between are freely traveled, we honor those who have come before and the Powers who guide them.  As the tree grows tall and strong from the foundation of the great Earth, so too do we grow wise by listening to the words of the Ancestors. All we are arises from all they have been. At this Samhain rite, let us hear the whispers of the Wise, let us walk the Old Ways well, and let the Spirits ward us tonight and throughout the rest of winter. We will call first upon he who goes before, Donn.

Honoring Donn

D1: In the season of darkening, the Lord of the House of Death receives the Spirits in his Hall.

      He is Donn the Dark One, the First Ancestor, the Torc Bearer, The Guardian of the Cauldron of Plenty. 

      You, who were the first to know Death, hear us now! 

      O Granter of Rest, Patron of the Feast in the Land of the Dead,  

      We your children pray that you gaze upon this Sacred Grove,

      And join us here to indwell our rite and grant us your blessings. To you, we make due offering.

 

We give you... Silver, that you grant the wealth of the Underworld, Source of All Potential.
[silver offering made into the well.]

Oil, that the richness of the Land be renewed as our own lives are renewed.
[oil offering made to the Fire]

And an apple, that those who know you may bless us as the world is still.
[apple offering placed at the foot of the Tree]

Be welcome with us, Dark One;

Donn, accept our offering!          ALL: Repeat


Honoring Cailleach Baere
PD2:  Now we call upon the Old Wise One, keeper of the mystery of life and death and the powers of the cauldron. 

 

D1:  As the Earth falls into sleep, the Crone of Baere is wielding her scythe and cutting the green corn down to sow it again in the late winter's night. She is the Cailleach, the wise and cunning old woman, who passed into seven ages of youth while her husbands passed away behind her.   Grandmother to peoples and races of the Earth, her eyes are keen and sparkling like the cold river stream, her hands are gnarled like the twisted roots of ancient trees, and her skin is old and wrinkled like the scattered leaves of autumn.

When the Ancient One calls from deep within the land, we know it in our bones. It is time to come home, into the darkness of our center, where the wise hag will cut us open, and we will grow new again as she does, our frailty and weakness cut down like the corn. Hear us now, Cailleach Mhor, Walker of the deepest, darkest Forest, Worker of the Roots and Cauldron. We your children pray that you gaze kindly upon this Sacred Grove and join us here, to indwell our rite and grant us your blessings. 

 

To you, we make due offering.  We give you...
Precious stone, that the Earth may be clothed again in vibrant life.
[stone offered into the well (red)]

Whiskey, that the Waters of Life May flow in us and Spirit indwell flesh.
[whiskey poured into the fire)]

Bone, that the old and unnecessary fall away into peaceful rest.
[bone placed at the base of the Tree]


Be welcome with us, Wizened Grandmother;

Cailleach Baere, accept our offering!       ALL: Repeat


Honoring the Ancestors

PD2: We now call to our Beloved Dead, the blessed Ancestors, to join our feast and receive due offering.

 

D1:  Come to the Gates, honored ones; hear our call, we your children remember you.

       We have gathered here to offer you our reverence and our love.

       We ask that you join us here to indwell our rite and grant us your blessings.

       Ancestors, you who fill the empty womb, you who cause the seed to spring,

       You, who fill the breast with milk, receive now these offerings, made in your honor:

We offer these apples, the fruit of life and death, to the heroes of Olde;
those men and women who sacrificed their lives for the good of the folk.
[apple offering made to the tree]

We offer this pork, the flesh of the Sacred Sow, to the honored Dead of this year;

Those whose stories will be told and remembered.
[pork offering made to the fire]

We offer these hazelnuts, the meat of wisdom, to our own Beloved Dead,
Family and friends who have gone ahead, we honor you and grieve for you.
[hazelnuts offering made to the well]

 

       Be welcome with us, Dear Ones.

       Ancestors, accept our offering.     ALL: Repeat


Main Offering
PD1:  Do you have Dear Ones to remember?   All: We do.
         Then take the time to remember them as we pass around this wreath of remembrance. Speak their names.

[rosemary wreath passed in the circle for spoken or silent praise]

Invocation of the Unknown Ancestors:

PD2: We call now to our Unknown Ancestors so that we may honor them and to come to know them.

 

A Litany [wreath held up near center as litany is read]

 

PD1:  To all those who died in childbirth,          All: We pay you homage. (repeat after each)

         To all those who died unborn,

         To all those who died in war,

         To all those who died making peace,

         To all those who died of illness,

         To all those who died on the journey to a new land,

         To all those who died of natural disaster,

         To all those who died from the failures of technology,

         To all those who died bringing knowledge, justice and goodness,

         To all those who died of a broken heart,

         To all those who died as innocent victims,

         To all those who died as strong survivors,

         To all those who died in hope,

         To all those who died in honor.     

 

Ancestors worthy of reverence, yet unknown –

We call to you here that you may be remembered now and always in the stories of our visions and dreams. 

May you feast in joy in the Land of the Dead.

 

Join us here, and accept our sacrifice!  [Food gathered earlier from potluck items and whiskey offered to well, (avoid salty foods)   rosemary wreath offered to fire] –

 

PD2:  If you have brought special offering for your Ancestors, please take the time to offer them now and to feel their presence.

 

As people are offering: Bards will sing – Into the West   

PD1: Let us now acknowledge the Ancestors yet to be, those who will travel through the threshold to die or to be born.  Let us offer them our blessings, our prayers, and our hopes for the future.  [Light from the fire and pass around white candle as blessings are spoken -  place in center of the circle when done.]



The Return Flow

Taking of the Omen
PD2: As we have offered with an open heart, let us know open further to hear the blessings and wisdom the Kindred wish to impart to us.   [Omen is taken]

Blessing Cup

PD2:  Shining Ones, Noble Ones, and Mighty Ones, we have given you praise and honor!
In the Ancient charge of hospitality, we pray you accept our sacrifices and grant us your blessings.

D1: Children of earth, we call for the blessing of the Ancient Ones.
      Together we say, Shining Ones, grant us the waters!
All: Shining Ones, grant us the Waters!

D2: We join our hearts, that each of us be blessed.
      Together we say, Shining Ones, grant us the waters!
All: Shining Ones, grant us the Waters!

D3:  And as we are blessed, let all the worlds be blessed.
       Together we pray, Shining Ones, grant us the waters!
All: Shining Ones, grant us the Waters!

PD1: Do you freely accept these blessings?   All: We do.

Then drink heartily! Seo iad uisci na beatha! ("shee ahd ishka nah bah-hah")
Behold, the Waters of Life!

[Pass the Blessing Cup Singing Pour the Waters 1x then drumming, music, and dance in celebration]

Thanking the Powers

PD2: We have called upon the Kindreds and they have answered us! As we prepare to bring the rite to a close, let us give thanks to those powers who have aided us.

D1: An Cailleach, Donn, and our beloved Ancestors, for the lessons you teach and the bond we hold dear,
go raibh maith agaibh! We thank you.
All: We thank you.

D: Dearest of allies, witnesses of this rite, who the folk gathered here to offer up praise,
go raibh maith agaibh. We thank you.

D: Shining Ones, for guiding our steps and this rite, and for holding the balance,
go raibh maith agaibh. We thank you.

D: Nature Spirits, for your beauty, strength, and presence here and in our lives,
go raibh maith agaibh. We thank you.

D: A Brid, Lady of the Hearth-Fires, for your inspiration and presence at this rite,
go raibh maith agat. We thank you.

D: A Lugh, strong straight spear, for the benevolent protection of your children,
go raibh maith agat. We thank you.

D: Dear One, called by many names as the Great Mother of the Earth, happy are we to be your children.
To you, we now return all of the blessings we could not carry here. Uphold us now in the world as you have done at this rite,
go raibh maith agat. We thank you!
[Offering back what is left of the blessing cup to the tree]


Regrounding and Centering
PD1: – Sending back to the Earth Mother and balancing the Two Powers for the mundane world once again.

Thanking and Petitioning the Gatekeeper

PD2: We call upon our Gatekeeper once more.

D: A Mhannain Mac Lir, Lord of the mists through which we travel,
for holding the gates open with us and keeping vigil over our steps,
Go raibh maith agat, we thank you.

Now, Ageless One, by your power and our magic we end what we began.
Let the well now be, the water that sustains us.
Let the fire now be, the flame that warms us.
Let the tree now be the shelter that comforts us.
Let all be as it was before.

A Mhannain Mac Lir, Dúntar na Geataí anois!
Let the Gates Be Closed!
ALL: Repeat.

Final Blessing and announcement of ending.
PD1: And as we started so let us begin.
[Chimes 3x3]


PD2: We have done as our ancestors did and as our children will do, and the Kindred have answered!
Let us move into the world secure in the knowledge that our offerings have pleased the gods and
that we go forth under their protection.

PD1:Bíodh sé amhlaidh!
